Achanakmar–Amarkantak Biosphere Reserve

Location & Area: Located in Chhattisgarh and Madhya Pradesh; covers ~3,835 km² including Achanakmar Wildlife Sanctuary and Amarkantak hills.

Geography & Habitat

This reserve lies at the junction of the Maikal Hills of the Satpuras. It features tropical moist deciduous forests, river valleys, and the source of the Narmada, Johilla, and Son rivers.

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Tigers, leopards, gaur, chital, sloth bear, dhole, and a wide range of reptiles and birds.
  • Flora: Rich sal forests, bamboo groves, and medicinal plants like harra, bahera, and amla.

Activities

  • Wildlife safaris in Achanakmar Sanctuary
  • Trekking in Amarkantak hills
  • Pilgrimage to origin sites of Narmada river

Accessibility & Permits

Nearest towns: Bilaspur (Chhattisgarh) and Amarkantak (Madhya Pradesh). Entry permits required for core areas of Achanakmar Sanctuary.

Significance

Declared a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve in 2005, it conserves unique biodiversity, protects the Narmada source, and sustains traditional tribal livelihoods.
