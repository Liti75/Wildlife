Pachmarhi Biosphere Reserve

Location & Area: Located in Madhya Pradesh, covering an area of about 4,926 km², including Satpura National Park, Bori Wildlife Sanctuary, and Pachmarhi Sanctuary.

Geography & Habitat

Situated in the Satpura Range, the reserve is characterized by rugged hills, valleys, plateaus, and rich forests with perennial rivers and waterfalls.

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Includes tigers, leopards, wild dogs, sloth bears, chitals, sambars, and diverse bird species.
  • Flora: Rich in teak, sal, bamboo, tendu, and medicinal plants.

Activities

  • Jungle safaris in Satpura National Park
  • Trekking and nature trails
  • Exploring waterfalls and caves of Pachmarhi

Accessibility & Permits

Nearest town: Pachmarhi. Accessible from Pipariya (55 km) and Bhopal (200 km). Entry permits are required for certain areas.

Significance

Recognized as a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve, it is crucial for tiger conservation and preserving central India’s biodiversity.
