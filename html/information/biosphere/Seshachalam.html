Seshachalam Hills Biosphere Reserve

Location & Area: Chittoor and Kadapa districts, Andhra Pradesh; covers about 4,755 km², designated as a biosphere reserve in 2010.

Geography & Habitat

The reserve encompasses part of the Eastern Ghats, with rugged hills, valleys, and seasonal rivers. The Seshachalam Hills are known for their unique red sanders forests.

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Leopard, sloth bear, golden jackal, jungle cat, sambar deer, spotted deer, Indian giant squirrel, monitor lizard, and numerous bird species.
  • Flora: Dominated by red sanders (Pterocarpus santalinus), tropical dry deciduous forests, and scrub vegetation.

Activities

  • Trekking and nature walks in forested hills
  • Birdwatching and wildlife photography
  • Exploration of sacred Tirumala hills and temples

Accessibility & Permits

Nearest city: Tirupati. Accessible via road and rail from major cities in Andhra Pradesh and Tamil Nadu. Permits may be needed for trekking in core forest areas.

Significance

The biosphere reserve is the only habitat for red sanders, a rare and valuable tree species found nowhere else in the world.
