Simlipal National Park

Location & Area: Mayurbhanj district, Odisha, India; spans around 2,750 km² with a core zone of ~1,195 km²

Overview & History

Simlipal, named for its abundance of red silk-cotton (Simuli) trees, was notified as a wildlife sanctuary in 1979 and declared a national park and tiger reserve by 1980–86. It forms part of the Mayurbhanj Elephant Reserve and was added to UNESCO’s Biosphere Reserves in 2009.

Topography & Landscape

Situated on an Eastern Ghats plateau at ~900 m elevation. Major peaks include Khariburu (1,178 m) and Meghasani (1,158 m). The park features two famous waterfalls: Barehipani (399 m, two-tier, India’s second highest) and Joranda (~150 m).

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Bengal tiger, Asian elephant, gaur, chausingha, leopards, sambar deer, and diverse birdlife including scarlet minivet, hornbills.
  • Flora: Mixed evergreen and deciduous forests dominated by sal, Semul (red silk-cotton), orchids, grasslands and riverside vegetation.

Visitor Info & Activities

  • Open from 1 November to 15 June. Closed during monsoon rains.
  • Access via Kaliani Gate (Jashipur) or Pithabata Gate (Baripada). Entry passes, safari and stay booked online or at gates.
  • Popular activities: jungle safaris, birdwatching, waterfall treks (to Barehipani & Joranda), and camping/nature stays at Gurguria, Barehipani, etc.

Significance

A key conservation zone in the Eastern Ghats, Simlipal plays a pivotal role in protecting elephants and tigers while preserving many endemic species and fragile ecosystems.
