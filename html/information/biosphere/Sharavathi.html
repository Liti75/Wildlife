Sharavathi Valley Biosphere Reserve

Location & Area: Located in the central Western Ghats, Shivamogga district, Karnataka; area ~905.6 km²; proposed as biosphere reserve in 2019.

Geography & Habitat

Sharavathi Valley includes riverine ecosystems, evergreen and semi-evergreen forests, and tropical rainforests. It is centered around the Sharavathi River and Jog Falls—the second-highest plunge waterfall in India.

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Lion-tailed macaque, tiger, leopard, sloth bear, wild boar, Indian giant squirrel, Malabar civet, king cobra, great Indian hornbill.
  • Flora: Dipterocarp trees, Myristica swamps, medicinal herbs, wild pepper, native orchids.

Activities

  • Trekking to Jog Falls, Kanoor Fort, and other trails
  • Wildlife and birdwatching tours in Muppane and Nagodi ranges
  • Nature education and eco-tourism camps

Accessibility & Permits

Nearest town: Sagar (30 km). Accessible via Shivamogga, Honnavar, and Mangalore. Permits may be required for restricted core zones and night stays near Jog Falls.

Significance

The region is ecologically sensitive and forms a part of the Western Ghats biodiversity hotspot. It safeguards many endemic species and supports hydroelectric power generation at Linganamakki Dam.
