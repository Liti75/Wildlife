Agasthyamalai Biosphere Reserve

Location & Area: Spread across Kerala and Tamil Nadu in the Western Ghats; covers ~3,500 km², with diverse forests and mountain ecosystems.

Geography & Habitat

This reserve is part of the southern Western Ghats, home to tropical evergreen forests, montane shola grasslands, and important watersheds. It includes Peppara, Neyyar, and Shendurney Wildlife Sanctuaries, and Kalakkad-Mundanthurai Tiger Reserve.

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Lion-tailed macaque, Nilgiri tahr, Indian elephant, tiger, leopard, Malabar civet, and hornbills.
  • Flora: Rich in endemic species like Agasthya tree fern, orchids, rare medicinal plants, and tropical rainforests.

Activities

  • Trekking to Agasthyakoodam peak
  • Birdwatching and wildlife safaris
  • Exploring Neyyar and Peppara sanctuaries

Accessibility & Permits

Nearest city: Thiruvananthapuram. Access via road and trekking trails. Special permits are required for trekking and entering certain core areas.

Significance

A UNESCO Biosphere Reserve, it is critical for biodiversity conservation, protecting endemic species, and preserving traditional tribal cultures.
