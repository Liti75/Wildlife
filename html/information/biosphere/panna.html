Panna Biosphere Reserve

Location & Area: Located in Madhya Pradesh, covering an area of about 2,998 km² including Panna National Park and surrounding forests.

Geography & Habitat

The reserve lies in the Vindhyan mountain range, characterized by plateaus, gorges, and river valleys, with the Ken River flowing through it.

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Includes tigers, leopards, chinkaras, nilgai, sloth bears, and over 200 bird species.
  • Flora: Dominated by dry deciduous forests with teak, tendu, mahua, and salai trees.

Activities

  • Wildlife safaris in Panna National Park
  • Boat rides on the Ken River
  • Birdwatching and photography

Accessibility & Permits

Nearest town: Panna. Accessible from Khajuraho (25 km) and Satna (90 km). Permits are required for park entry.

Significance

Recognized for tiger conservation under Project Tiger and as a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve, it is vital for biodiversity in central India.
