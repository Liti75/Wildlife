Darjeeling Himalayan Biosphere Reserve

Location & Area: Situated in West Bengal, covering ~2,620 km² of the Eastern Himalayas including forests, alpine meadows, and river valleys.

Geography & Habitat

The reserve encompasses high-altitude ecosystems ranging from subtropical forests to alpine vegetation. It includes Singalila National Park and Senchal Wildlife Sanctuary.

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Red panda, Himalayan black bear, snow leopard, clouded leopard, and over 500 bird species.
  • Flora: Oaks, rhododendrons, bamboo, magnolia, and alpine grasses dominate the landscape.

Activities

  • Trekking in Singalila National Park
  • Birdwatching and nature trails
  • Exploring alpine and rhododendron forests

Accessibility & Permits

Nearest town: Darjeeling. Easily accessible from Siliguri via road. Entry permits may be required for national parks within the reserve.

Significance

A UNESCO Biosphere Reserve, it is vital for conserving high-altitude Himalayan biodiversity and supporting eco-tourism in the region.
