Nanda Devi Biosphere Reserve

Location & Area: Located in Uttarakhand, India; covering ~5,860 km² in the Himalayas, surrounding the Nanda Devi peak.

Geography & Habitat

The reserve encompasses the Nanda Devi National Park and Valley of Flowers, with alpine meadows, glaciers, and rugged Himalayan terrain.

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Snow leopard, Himalayan musk deer, bharal (blue sheep), Himalayan tahr, and numerous bird species.
  • Flora: Alpine flora, rhododendrons, junipers, birch, and endemic flowering plants in the Valley of Flowers.

Activities

  • Trekking in Valley of Flowers
  • Himalayan wildlife observation
  • Scenic views of Nanda Devi peak and glaciers

Accessibility & Permits

Nearest town: Joshimath. Accessible via road from Rishikesh and Haridwar; trekking required to reach core zones. Entry permits are mandatory.

Significance

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, vital for high-altitude biodiversity conservation and Himalayan ecology.
