Bhagwan Mahavir Wildlife Sanctuary & Mollem National Park

Location & Area: Goa’s largest protected area (~240 km²), declared 1969, lies along the Western Ghats within Sanguem and Dharbandora Talukas. Mollem National Park (107 km²) forms its core. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

Geography & Habitat

Dominated by West Coast tropical evergreen, semi‑evergreen and moist deciduous forests. Waterways and perennial river stretches support lush vegetation. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Tigers, leopards, sloth bear, gaur, sambar, barking deer, Malayan giant squirrel, slender loris, bonnet macaque, otter, over 120 bird species. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}
  • Flora: Over 722 flowering plant species (128 endemics) including Terminalia, Xylia, Lagerstroemia, Dalbergia. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

Activities

  • Dudhsagar waterfall trek, jeep safaris, birding & wildlife photography near Tambdi Surla temple and Devil’s Canyon viewpoint. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

Accessibility & Permits

About 53 km from Panaji; open daily 08:00–17:30. Entry ₹20/adult, ₹10/child; ₹30 still camera, ₹150 video camera. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}

Significance

Largest protected area in Goa and key biodiversity zone in Western Ghats, supporting flagship fauna and endemic flora. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}
