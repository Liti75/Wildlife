Mhadei Wildlife Sanctuary

Location & Area: Located in North Goa (Sattari taluka near Valpoi), it spans ~208 km²; declared in 1999. Goa thus protects the full stretch of Western Ghats within the state. :contentReference[oaicite:30]{index=30}

Geography & Habitat

Hilly terrain with peaks like Vagheri (~1027 m), forested slopes, sacred groves, waterfalls (Vazra Sakla, Virdi), and river Mhadei catchment area. :contentReference[oaicite:31]{index=31}

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Tiger, leopard, sloth bear, gaur, barking deer, mouse deer, slender loris, pangolin, ~255 bird species incl. Malabar parakeet, grey-headed bulbul, crimson-backed sunbird; >330 butterfly species. :contentReference[oaicite:32]{index=32}
  • Flora: Moist deciduous and evergreen forest, rare Ashoka species, sacred groves; high diversity of orchids, caecilians amphibians, and snakes including all “Big Four” venomous types. :contentReference[oaicite:33]{index=33}

Activities

  • Hiking in Vagheri Hills and Chorla Ghat, bird‑butterfly watching, white water rafting during monsoon season. :contentReference[oaicite:34]{index=34}

Accessibility & Permits

Accessible via Valpoi and Chorla; forest department permits required. Human-animal conflict noted due to pending tribal claims. :contentReference[oaicite:35]{index=35}

Significance

Key biodiversity zone—a prospective Project Tiger reserve, with endemic fauna, sacred groves and endemic amphibians. :contentReference[oaicite:36]{index=36}
