Spike Island‑I Wildlife Sanctuary

Location & Area: Part of coastal reef systems in North Andaman, Spike Island‑I covers ~0.42 km² and was designated as a sanctuary in 1987.:contentReference[oaicite:11]{index=11}

Geography & Habitat

A small reef island surrounded by coral formations and shallow marine zones, providing habitat for nesting seabirds and reef fish.

Fauna & Flora

  • Fauna: Seabirds, reef fish, marine invertebrates and occasional turtle sightings.
  • Flora: Seagrass patches, algae, coastal shrubs on the reef fringe.

Activities

  • Marine biodiversity surveys (research-only access)
  • Bird monitoring and coral ecology studies

Accessibility & Permits

Access is restricted—entry only by permit through the forest department; general visitation is not allowed.:contentReference[oaicite:12]{index=12}

Significance

A vital micro-sanctuary safeguarding sensitive marine and avifaunal ecosystems within the Andaman region.:contentReference[oaicite:13]{index=13}
