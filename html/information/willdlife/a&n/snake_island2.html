Snake Island‑I Wildlife Sanctuary

Location & Area: A small reef island off Port Blair, notified as a wildlife sanctuary in 1987, spanning approximately 0.73 km². :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

Geography & Habitat

An uninhabited rocky reef island with coral zones and sandy coves. It supports pristine reef systems and diverse underwater marine life close to Port Blair. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Reef fish, invertebrates, occasional sea turtles, seabirds
  • Flora: Seagrasses, marine algae, coral formations

Activities

  • Snorkeling and scuba diving (with permit from authorities) :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}
  • Marine biodiversity observation and underwater photography

Accessibility & Permits

Accessible by boat from Port Blair (about 45–60 min). A permit from the Andaman Forest Department is mandatory for visitation. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

Significance

Critical site for coral reef ecology and conservation, located near the capital region of the islands. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}
