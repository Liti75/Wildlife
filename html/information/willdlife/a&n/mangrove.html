Mangrove Island Wildlife Sanctuary

Location & Area: Established in 1987; covers approx 0.39 km² on Mangrove Island in North Andaman.:contentReference[oaicite:12]{index=12}

Geography & Habitat

Predominantly mangrove forest ecosystem with tidal creeks, muddy flats and coastal biodiversity.:contentReference[oaicite:13]{index=13}

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Coastal birds, reptiles, fish and invertebrates.
  • Flora: Mangrove species endemic to Andaman wetlands.:contentReference[oaicite:14]{index=14}

Activities

  • Mangrove boat safari
  • Cooking and learning about traditional mangrove ecology
  • Photography of birds and intertidal life

Accessibility & Permits

Reachable via boat from North Andaman coastal stations; forest permits required.:contentReference[oaicite:15]{index=15}

Significance

Important micro‑sanctuary preserving rare mangrove ecosystems and associated biodiversity.:contentReference[oaicite:16]{index=16}
