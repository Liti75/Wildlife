Table (Delgarno) Island Wildlife Sanctuary

Location & Area: Off Diglipur, North & Middle Andaman; ~2.29 km²; declared in 1987. Known locally as “Table Delgarno” Island WLS :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

Geography & Habitat

A low-lying reef‑fringed island in the Table Group, featuring sandy shores, lagoons and coral formations resembling an atoll environment :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}.

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Reef fish, nesting seabirds, marine invertebrates.
  • Flora: Coral reef species, seagrasses, coastal shrubs.

Activities

  • Marine surveys, seabird monitoring (restricted access only)
  • Photography for research purposes (permit required)

Accessibility & Permits

Permit-only access via the Forest Department; no tourism facilities present.

Significance

Part of the ecologically significant Table Group of islands, often dubbed Andaman’s “Maldives” for its lagoon‑like reefs and sandbanks :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}.
