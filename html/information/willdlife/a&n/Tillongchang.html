Tillongchang Island Wildlife Sanctuary

Location & Area: A small reef island sanctuary in North Andaman, approximately 0.05 km², designated in 1987.

Geography & Habitat

A tiny coral-fringed islet with sandy beaches and shallow reef zones, mostly uninhabited.

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Reef fish, seabirds and occasional turtle activity.
  • Flora: Coastal shrubs, reef-edge algae and seagrass.

Activities

  • Scientific reef monitoring and seabird surveys (restricted access)

Accessibility & Permits

Only accessible via permit from the Andaman Forest Department; public visits prohibited.

Significance

One of the smallest sanctuaries, critical for a reef‑based micro‑ecosystem protection.
