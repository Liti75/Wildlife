Megapode Island Wildlife Sanctuary

Location & Area: Located near Great Nicobar, named for the Nicobar megapode (Megapodius nicobariensis); officially denotified in January 2021, previously a protected sanctuary. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

Geography & Habitat

Consists of mangroves, scrub forest and coastal terrain—ideal nesting ground for the megapode bird. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Nicobar megapode, reef birds, turtles
  • Flora: Coastal shrub and mangrove communities supporting nesting habitat

Activities

  • Observation of megapode nesting sites (limited access)
  • Ecological research on endemic bird behavior

Accessibility & Permits

Previously restricted area; access via forest department and Indian Navy permissions. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}

Significance

One of the only global habitats for Nicobar megapode; conservation significance high despite de-notification. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}
