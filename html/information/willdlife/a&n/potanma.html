Potanma Islands Wildlife Sanctuary

Location & Area: Located in the Andaman Islands, this sanctuary covers a small coral and reef island of ~0.16 km², designated in 1987.

Geography & Habitat

The sanctuary lies within the Andaman group, protecting coral reefs, nesting seabirds, and sensitive marine ecosystems. It includes intertidal zones and reef lagoons.

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Predominantly seabirds, reef-associated marine fauna like reef fish, crustaceans, and mollusks.
  • Flora: Coral species, seagrass beds, and coastal shrubs adapted to the saline environment.

Activities

  • Research on coral reef ecosystems
  • Monitoring seabird nesting colonies
  • Photography and eco-tourism (restricted)

Accessibility & Permits

Accessible by boat with special permission from the Andaman Forest Department. Limited access due to its sensitive ecological status.

Significance

This is one of the smallest yet most ecologically valuable sanctuaries, highlighting India's commitment to protecting marine biodiversity and reef systems.
