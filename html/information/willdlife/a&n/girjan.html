Girjan Island Wildlife Sanctuary

Location & Area: Andaman & Nicobar Islands; notified in 1987 with area around 0.16 km²—designated for reef and seabird conservation. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}

Geography & Habitat

A small coral-reef islet supporting littoral vegetation and seabird nesting zones in the Andaman archipelago. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Reef-nesting birds, small coastal reptiles and marine fauna in surrounding waters. :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}
  • Flora: Salt-tolerant shrubs, littoral grasses inhabiting small island shores.

Activities

  • Seabird and coastal ecosystem observation
  • Photography of reef-form islet and birdlife

Accessibility & Permits

Boat access only; strict conservation restrictions—visitors must obtain permit from authorities to protect nesting areas.

Significance

Part of the network of tiny islet sanctuaries protecting coral-reef and seabird habitat across Andaman Islands. :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}
