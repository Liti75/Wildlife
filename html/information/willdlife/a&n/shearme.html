Shearme Island Wildlife Sanctuary

Location & Area: Located off North Andaman, Shearme Island Wildlife Sanctuary was established in 1987 and covers approximately 7.85 km² :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}.

Geography & Habitat

Shearme Island is a coral‑fringed island sanctuary with beaches, rocky outcrops and dense coastal vegetation. It supports both terrestrial nesting sites and marine ecosystems :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}.

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Seabirds, reef fish, crustaceans, and nesting marine turtles.
  • Flora: Mangroves, seagrasses, littoral shrubs and coral reefs.

Activities

  • Marine research and coral reef assessment
  • Bird nesting surveys and ecological monitoring
  • Photography and limited eco‑tourism (with permit)

Accessibility & Permits

Access is restricted and regulated by the Andaman Forest Department and environmental authorities. Only researchers and permit holders may enter.

Significance

One of the larger reef‑island sanctuaries in Andaman, it plays a significant role in seabird nesting protection and coral reef conservation :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}.
