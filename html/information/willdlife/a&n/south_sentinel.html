South Sentinel Island Wildlife Sanctuary

Location & Area: South Sentinel Island lies in South Andaman, covering ~1.61 km² and was declared a sanctuary in 1977. It's a remote coral‑fringed island with dense forest and lagoons.:contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

Geography & Habitat

A flat coral island featuring intertidal lagoons, rocky beaches, and dense evergreen forest canopy. The central marsh fills with tidal water.:contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

Fauna & Flora

  • Fauna: Nesting green and leatherback turtles, coconut crabs, Andaman day gecko, monitor lizards, saltwater crocodiles, nesting pigeons and Andaman crake.:contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}
  • Flora: Tropical evergreen forest, littoral vegetation and dune‑adapted shrubs along the shoreline.

Activities

  • Turtle nesting observation (seasonal research)
  • Bird and reptile surveys (authorized only)

Accessibility & Permits

Strictly regulated—access only for researchers with prior government clearance; public visitation is prohibited.:contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}

Significance

This sanctuary protects one of the last remaining pristine coral island ecosystems in the region and serves as critical nesting grounds for endangered marine species.:contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}
