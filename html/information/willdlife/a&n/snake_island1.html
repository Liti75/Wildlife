Snake Island‑I Wildlife Sanctuary

Location & Area: Located off Port Blair, this sanctuary was established in 1987 and covers approx. 0.73 km² :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}.

Geography & Habitat

An uninhabited rocky reef island with sandy coves and coral areas. Known for pristine reef systems and rich underwater biodiversity. It lies just off Corbyn’s Cove in Port Blair :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}.

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Reef fish, marine invertebrates, occasional sea turtles, seabirds.
  • Flora: Seagrasses, coastal shrubs, reef corals and algae.

Activities

  • Scuba diving and snorkeling (permit required)
  • Marine biodiversity studies and reef monitoring
  • Photography and restricted ecological tours

Accessibility & Permits

Access is restricted and regulated by authorities. A permit via the Andaman Forest Department is mandatory for visitors and dive operators :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}.

Significance

Serves as a critical site for coral reef ecology and marine life protection near the capital region of Andaman and Nicobar Islands.
