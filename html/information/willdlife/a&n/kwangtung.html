Kwangtung Island Wildlife Sanctuary

Location & Area: North Andaman Island; notified in 1987, area ~0.57 km² under IUCN category IV protecting coastal biodiversity. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}

Geography & Habitat

A small coastal island southeast of McPherson Bay, featuring marine-fringed habitat with coral reefs and mangrove patches. Managed under Andaman Forest Department guidelines. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Nicobar pigeon, Andaman teal, saltwater crocodile, marine turtles, water monitor, wild boar. :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}
  • Flora: Coastal trees like Dipterocarpus, Ficus retusa, Pongamia pinnata, Terminalia bialata and mangrove vegetation. :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}

Activities

  • Birdwatching of endemic and migratory species
  • Photography of coastal wildlife and reef ecosystems

Accessibility & Permits

Boat access from North Andaman; visitor access restricted—permits required for wildlife observation or research purposes. :contentReference[oaicite:11]{index=11}

Significance

Part of Andaman’s coastal protected area network safeguarding rare bird species and marine fauna in reef‑mangrove island ecosystems. :contentReference[oaicite:12]{index=12}
