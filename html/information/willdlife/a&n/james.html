James Island Wildlife Sanctuary

Location & Area: South Andaman, Andaman Islands; notified in 1987, area ~2.1 km². Protected as a bird‑island sanctuary. Established to conserve coastal and lagoon ecosystems. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

Geography & Habitat

Situated within a lagoon system off Andaman mainland, James Island is a small uninhabited island hosting coastal vegetation and migratory seabirds. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Reef‑nesting seabirds, coastal reptiles, marine turtles, and shorebirds typical of lagoon habitats. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}
  • Flora: Salt‑tolerant coastal shrubs, grassland flora and mangrove fringes around lagoon shores.

Activities

  • Seabird and shorebird observation
  • Photography of coastal lagoon and birdlife

Accessibility & Permits

Accessible by boat from Port Blair regions; visits require forest‑department permission to protect nesting zones.

Significance

Important small island sanctuary in Andaman’s network of bird‑islands; critical for migratory seabird habitat in lagoon ecosystems. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}
