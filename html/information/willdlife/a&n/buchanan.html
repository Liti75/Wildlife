Buchanan Island Wildlife Sanctuary

Location & Area: Located in the Andaman group, this sanctuary spans approximately 9.33 km². Gazetted in 1987 as one of the island sanctuaries protecting coastal and reef biodiversity. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

Geography & Habitat

Buchanan is a coral‑fringed island with gentle reef edges and coastal scrub vegetation typical of Andaman islets. It forms part of an archipelago of small protected islands established to protect marine and bird life.

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Reef‑associated seabirds, coral reef fish, nesting turtles, and coastal wildlife.
  • Flora: Littoral shrubs, coastal grasses, and reef-edge vegetation.

Activities

  • Birdwatching for seabirds and coastal species
  • Snorkeling or reef observation (from permitted boats)
  • Nature photography of island and marine scenery

Accessibility & Permits

Buchanan Island is accessed by boat from nearby islands; entry is restricted and requires permit from the Forest Department.

Significance

One of larger small‑island sanctuaries in the Andaman group, it plays a vital role in preserving island marine ecosystems including coral reefs, seabird breeding grounds, and coastal flora.
