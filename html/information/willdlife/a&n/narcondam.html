Narcondam Island Wildlife Sanctuary

Location & Area: Covers the entire Narcondam volcanic island (~6.81 km²); designated to protect the endemic Narcondam hornbill. :contentReference[oaicite:14]{index=14}

Geography & Habitat

A remote uninhabited volcanic island featuring wet evergreen forest on higher slopes and beach vegetation near the coast. Only resident humans are security staff. :contentReference[oaicite:15]{index=15}

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Endemic Narcondam hornbill (only known wild population), Andaman water monitor, paradise flying snake, blue-lipped sea krait. :contentReference[oaicite:16]{index=16}
  • Flora: Evergreen and moist deciduous species; grassy slopes on upper elevation. :contentReference[oaicite:17]{index=17}

Activities

  • Strict research and limited wildlife monitoring only

Accessibility & Permits

Access heavily restricted; requires special permission from environment, defense, and forest departments. :contentReference[oaicite:18]{index=18}

Significance

One of the world’s most restricted bird conservation sites; hornbill with extremely limited range makes it a global priority for biodiversity protection. :contentReference[oaicite:19]{index=19}
