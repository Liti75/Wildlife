Sisters Island Wildlife Sanctuary

Location & Area: Consists of two uninhabited reef islands (East & West Sister) in South Andaman, covering approx. 0.36 km². Declared a sanctuary in 1987 to protect seabird nesting and reef environments. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

Geography & Habitat

Located about 57 km south of Port Blair in the Rutland Archipelago, the Sister Islands are connected by a coral reef. The terrain includes rocky shores, small beaches, and dense coastal forest. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Important nesting grounds for seabirds such as terns and gulls; reef fish and marine invertebrates inhabit the surrounding lagoon waters.
  • Flora: Coastal forests and corals, with seagrasses and littoral shrubs along the shoreline. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

Activities

  • Seabird nesting monitoring (scientific research)
  • Reef ecology surveys and marine research
  • Restricted photography (permit mandatory)

Accessibility & Permits

Access is highly restricted and controlled by the Department of Environment & Forests, Andaman & Nicobar Islands. Only authorized researchers are allowed with permits; general tourism is prohibited. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

Significance

Key sanctuary for conserving breeding seabird populations and protecting unspoiled coral reef ecosystems in the region. Its small size highlights the importance of micro-habitats in marine biodiversity conservation. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}
