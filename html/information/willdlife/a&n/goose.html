Goose Island Wildlife Sanctuary

Location & Area: Located in North Andaman; notified 16 February 1987 covering ~0.01 km². Established to protect seabird habitat, marine turtles and coastal biodiversity. :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}

Geography & Habitat

A tiny vegetated reef‑islet surrounded by coral reefs and open sea. Habitat for seabirds, marine turtles and littoral wildlife common in North Andaman. :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Nicobar pigeon, Andaman crake, Andaman tern, serpent‑eagle; olive ridley and hawksbill turtles occasionally nest nearby. :contentReference[oaicite:11]{index=11}
  • Flora: Coastal forest clearings with Andaman padauk, Pandanus, Ficus retusa, Terminalia bialata, Pongamia pinnata. :contentReference[oaicite:12]{index=12}

Activities

  • Birdwatching – seabirds and rare island species
  • Photography of coral‑reef islet and coastal vegetation

Accessibility & Permits

Accessible by boat from North Andaman mainland; permits required due to restricted habitat and nesting sites. :contentReference[oaicite:13]{index=13}

Significance

One of the smallest protected areas, yet valuable for preserving nesting bird colonies and supporting marine turtle activity in Andaman region. :contentReference[oaicite:14]{index=14}
