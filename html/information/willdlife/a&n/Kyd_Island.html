Kyd Island Wildlife Sanctuary

Location & Area: Kyd Island Wildlife Sanctuary is located in South Andaman district, part of the Napier Bay Islands. It covers an area of approximately 8 km². The sanctuary was notified in 1987 to protect marine, bird, and coastal ecosystems.

Geography & Habitat

Kyd Island lies north of Shoal Bay and features undisturbed tropical island terrain, coastal forests, and coral reef surroundings. It hosts a small hill (~237 m elevation), making it a significant ecological niche for birds and marine life.

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Coastal and migratory seabirds, marine turtles, saltwater crocodiles, fish species, and reef-dwelling fauna.
  • Flora: Littoral forests, mangroves, and salt-tolerant coastal plant species. Coral reefs surround the island’s perimeter.

Activities

  • Birdwatching and wildlife observation
  • Snorkeling near coral reefs (requires permission)
  • Eco-photography and nature documentation

Accessibility & Permits

The sanctuary is accessible by boat from Port Blair. Entry is restricted and requires prior permission from the Department of Environment & Forests, Andaman & Nicobar Islands.

Significance

Kyd Island is part of the protected coral island ecosystem in the Bay of Bengal. It plays a crucial role in marine conservation and bird nesting habitats, particularly for migratory species.
