Temple Island Wildlife Sanctuary

Location & Area: A small reef island sanctuary within North Andaman’s wildlife network; area approx. 3.07 km² (similar islands in region are ~3 km²) and notified in 1987 :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}.

Geography & Habitat

Reef‑fringed islet with sandy beaches and shallow lagoon systems. Supports coral ecosystems and nesting seabirds.

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Reef fish, seabirds, turtles and coastal invertebrates.
  • Flora: Seagrasses, coral reef vegetation and scrub shrubs.

Activities

  • Limited research into marine ecology
  • Bird monitoring and reef surveys by permit only

Accessibility & Permits

Strictly restricted; researchers must obtain permission from the Forest Department; no public access.

Significance

One of many reef‑island sanctuaries that safeguard marine‑avian biodiversity in North Andaman’s archipelago cluster.
