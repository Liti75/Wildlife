Turtle Islands Wildlife Sanctuary

Location & Area: Located in Tawi‑Tawi, Philippines, straddling the Malaysia–Philippines border. Covers ~2,429 km² and designated a sanctuary in August 1999 :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}.

Geography & Habitat

Comprises six coral islands with sandy beaches and near‑shore reef systems. Important nesting zones for marine turtles and rich coastal biodiversity :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}.

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Green turtles, olive ridley turtles, hawksbill turtles; nesting beaches support major egg-laying colonies.
  • Flora: Coastal shrubs, dune vegetation, and reef-associated marine flora.

Activities

  • Conservation research and turtle nesting monitoring
  • Restricted eco-tourism under bilateral management

Accessibility & Permits

Entry is tightly regulated under ASEAN-coordinated management. Access generally limited to conservation personnel and approved researchers.

Significance

One of the world’s most significant turtle nesting sites, jointly managed by Malaysia and the Philippines. Vital for marine turtle conservation in Southeast Asia.
