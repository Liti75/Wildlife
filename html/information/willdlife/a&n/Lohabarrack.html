Lohabarrack (Saltwater Crocodile) Wildlife Sanctuary

Location & Area: Situated ~30 km north of Port Blair in South Andaman; established in 1987, spanning approx 22.21 km². Primarily for saltwater crocodile conservation.:contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}

Geography & Habitat

Dominated by dense mangrove forests and estuarine wetlands adjacent to sandy creek zones.:contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Saltwater crocodile, estuarine birds, fish, small mammals.
  • Flora: Tropical mangrove species and wetland vegetation.:contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}

Activities

  • Guided mangrove walks
  • Crocodile spotting from safe vantage points
  • Birdwatching in wetland zones

Accessibility & Permits

Access via forest‑department boat rides from Port Blair; permits required.:contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}

Significance

The only sanctuary in the archipelago explicitly created to preserve saltwater crocodiles and associated mangrove–wetland ecosystem.:contentReference[oaicite:11]{index=11}
