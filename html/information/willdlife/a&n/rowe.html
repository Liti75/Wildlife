Rowe Island Wildlife Sanctuary

Location & Area: Located in the Andaman Islands, Rowe Island Wildlife Sanctuary covers around 0.33 km² and was officially notified in 1987.

Geography & Habitat

Rowe Island is surrounded by coral reefs and shallow lagoons. It features sandy shores, rocky outcrops, and lush green vegetation inland.

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Noted for hosting seabird nesting colonies, particularly terns and frigatebirds; the reefs support reef fish and juvenile turtles.
  • Flora: Mangroves along the shoreline and patches of tropical shrubland and grasses inland.

Activities

  • Field surveys and ecological studies (restricted access)
  • Seabird nesting monitoring
  • Conservation research and photography (with permit)

Accessibility & Permits

Access requires special permission from the Department of Environment & Forests, A&N Islands. It is not open for general tourism due to its ecological sensitivity.

Significance

One of the smaller island sanctuaries that plays a crucial role in nesting bird conservation and in maintaining coastal marine biodiversity.
