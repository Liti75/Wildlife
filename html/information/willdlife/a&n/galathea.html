Galathea Bay Wildlife Sanctuary

Location & Area: On the southern tip of Great Nicobar Island; declared ~11.44 km² in 1997 as a wildlife sanctuary. Famous as India’s largest nesting site for the endangered leatherback turtle. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

Geography & Habitat

Includes coastal rainforest, mangrove-fringed marshlands, and silver-sands at the mouth of the Galathea River. Surrounding coral reefs and tidal lagoons support rich marine life. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Leatherback, Olive Ridley and Green turtles; saltwater crocodiles; dugong; Nicobar crab‑eating macaque; Nicobar flying fox; megapode; endemic birds. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}
  • Flora: Tropical evergreen forests, mangroves (Rhizophora, Bruguiera, Sonneratia), tree‑ferns, orchids, and endemic plants including Mesua ferrea, Pterocarpus indicus, Calophyllum species. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

Activities

  • Turtle‑nesting observation during November–March
  • Wildlife‑photography of dugongs, crocodiles, endemic birds
  • Nature‑trekking in rainforest zones (with permissions only)

Accessibility & Permits

Remote location reachable only by multi‑day sea voyage from Port Blair to Campbell Bay. Access restricted and requires special permits due to ecological sensitivity. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}

Significance

Pivotal nesting beach for the world’s largest turtle, the leatherback. Denotified in 2021 but remains critical for conservation of sea turtles and endemic species in Great Nicobar. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}
