North Island Wildlife Sanctuary

Location & Area: Located on North Island in the Great Andaman group, approx 0.49 km²; designated in 1987. Safeguards coastal and moist broadleaf forest ecosystems. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

Geography & Habitat

North Island features tropical and subtropical moist broadleaf forest, expansive beaches, coral reefs, and mangrove patches. It lies just north of Middle Andaman and faces Arial Bay. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Andaman wild pig, imperial pigeon, Andaman crake, serpent‑eagle, Andaman teal, and marine turtles. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}
  • Flora: Bamboo, Andaman padauk (Pterocarpus dalbergioides), bullet wood, Pandanus, Ficus retusa, Manilkara littoralis, Pongamia pinnata. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

Activities

  • Wildlife and birdwatching (notably endemic and island species)
  • Guided coastal walks and eco‑studies in permitted zones

Accessibility & Permits

Accessible by boat from adjacent islands (e.g. Middle or Little Andaman); entry requires forest department permissions. Only day visits permitted under supervised conditions. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}

Significance

Important micro‑sanctuary supporting endemic Andaman fauna and vital coastal forest ecosystems. Integral to the region’s conservation network of small island sanctuaries. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}
