North Island Wildlife Sanctuary

Location & Area: Situated on North Island, Great Andaman group; established in 1987, covering ~0.49 km². :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}

Geography & Habitat

Lush, tropical island featuring moist broadleaf forest, coastal fringes, bamboo understorey and Pandanus growths. Nearly flat overall with sandy beaches and dense interior vegetation. :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Andaman wild pig, Andaman hill myna, imperial pigeon, water monitor, various bird species and small mammals. :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}
  • Flora: Bamboo, Andaman padauk, bullet wood, Ficus, Pandanus and other coastal forest plant species. :contentReference[oaicite:11]{index=11}

Activities

  • Wildlife observation and botanical studies
  • Low-impact eco‑tourism and guided walks within permitted zones

Accessibility & Permits

Access allowed under forest department supervision; requires permits and boat passage from Little or Middle Andaman islands. :contentReference[oaicite:12]{index=12}

Significance

Important for conservation of several endemic Andaman species, and part of a network of micro‑sanctuaries preserving biodiversity across island groups. :contentReference[oaicite:13]{index=13}
