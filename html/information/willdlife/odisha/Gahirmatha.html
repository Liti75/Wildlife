Gahirmatha Marine Wildlife Sanctuary

Location & Area: Kendrapara District, Odisha; area ~1,435 km² (1,408 km² sea + 27 km² land), established September 1997. It extends from Dhamra River mouth (north) to Brahmani River mouth (south) :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}.

Geography & Habitat

A coastal marine sanctuary bordering Bhitarkanika National Park, including sandy nesting beaches, tidal flats and adjacent ocean zones :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}.

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Globally largest olive ridley turtle rookery; nesting numbers exceed 100,000 annually. Other wildlife: wild boar, barking deer, crocodiles, bear, leopard, jungle fowl, sambar, wild dog :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}.
  • Flora: Coastal and mangrove-associated trees like Sal, Bija, Dhaura, Casia, Bamboo, Teak near fringes :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}.

Activities

  • Mass turtle nesting (arribada) observation November–May
  • Guided nature boat trips near Bhitarkanika mangrove zones

Accessibility & Permits

Accessible via Chandbali and Rajnagar; boat access required to reach nesting areas. Permits via Odisha Forest Department for eco‑tour visits :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}.

Significance

India’s only marine wildlife sanctuary; vital conservation site for olive ridley sea turtles and coastal marine biodiversity. Featured in global conservation studies and ongoing tagging programs :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}.
