Karlapat Wildlife Sanctuary

Location & Area: Kalahandi district, Odisha; covers ~147.7 km² (some sources cite ~175–255 km²). Declared on 2 April 1992. IUCN Category IV. :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}

Geography & Habitat

Located ~15 km from Bhawanipatna; undulating terrain of Eastern Highlands moist deciduous forests, rocky hills and seasonal streams including Phurlijharan waterfall. :contentReference[oaicite:11]{index=11}

Flora & Fauna

  • Flora: Mixed deciduous forest of Sal, Asan, Amala, Harida, Bija, bamboo, medicinal species. :contentReference[oaicite:12]{index=12}
  • Fauna: Leopard, tiger, gaur, sambar, nilgai, barking deer, mouse-deer, otters, diverse reptiles and ~150+ bird species. :contentReference[oaicite:13]{index=13}

Activities

  • Trekking, waterfall picnicking (Phurlijharan), nature walks, wildlife photography. :contentReference[oaicite:14]{index=14}

Accessibility & Permits

Closest town Bhawanipatna; ~15 – 35 km away. Accessible by road; drive-in nature tourism facilities available; local guides recommended. :contentReference[oaicite:15]{index=15}

Significance

A lesser-known Eastern Ghats biodiversity hotspot, conserving large mammals and tribal forest habitats. Offbeat destination with relatively low visitor pressure. :contentReference[oaicite:16]{index=16}
