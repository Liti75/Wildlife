Sunabeda Wildlife Sanctuary

Location & Area: Nuapada District bordering Chhattisgarh; ~600 km²; established 1988; under consideration as tiger reserve (~956 km² incl. buffer) :contentReference[oaicite:34]{index=34}.

Geography & Habitat

Plateau and canyon terrain with waterfalls (11+), catchment of Jonk River dam, dry deciduous tropical forest including teak, Bija, Mahul and bamboo :contentReference[oaicite:35]{index=35}.

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Tigers, leopards, swamp deer (barasingha), wild buffalo corridor, hyena, gaur, sambar, chital, sloth bear, barasingha; rich birdlife including Indian paradise flycatcher, Indian pitta, cuckoos and forest owlet in potential presence :contentReference[oaicite:36]{index=36}.
  • Flora: Mixed dry deciduous species: teak, sisoo, Pterocarpus, Terminalia, Anogeissus, Santalum, etc. :contentReference[oaicite:37]{index=37}.

Activities

  • Wildlife trails, birdwatching, waterfall treks, and tribal cultural exposure around Daringbadi and Nuapada :contentReference[oaicite:38]{index=38}.

Accessibility & Permits

About 140 km from Phulbani/Baliguda; permits required for forest entry and deeper exploration; eco‑lodging options limited.

Significance

Important tiger‑elephant corridor and swamp‑deer habitat; vital for megafauna movement between Odisha and Chhattisgarh :contentReference[oaicite:39]{index=39}.
