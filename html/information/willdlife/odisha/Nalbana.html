Nalbana Bird Sanctuary (Chilika Lake)

Location & Area: Center of Chilika Lake, Puri District; ~15.53 km². Declared a bird sanctuary in 1973; core of the Ramsar‑designated wetland from 1987 :contentReference[oaicite:14]{index=14}.

Geography & Habitat

A seasonal island emerging from Chilika Lake post‑monsoon, featuring extensive mudflats vital for migratory waders :contentReference[oaicite:15]{index=15}.

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Greater & lesser flamingos, Goliath heron, various storks (Dalmatian pelican, spoon‑billed sandpiper, ibis), egrets, waders; nesting colonies of river terns and gull‑billed terns :contentReference[oaicite:16]{index=16}.
  • Flora: Mudflat vegetation and algae supporting migratory bird feeding grounds.

Activities

  • Birdwatching during winter migration; boat cruises across Chilika lagoon and guided tours via Chilika Development Authority.

Accessibility & Permits

Accessible via Puri or Rambha; boat transport required; eco‑tour options available. Entry free within Chilika cruise service framework.

Significance

Major migratory birding site in Asia; supports several threatened species and Southeast Asia’s largest river tern nesting colony.
