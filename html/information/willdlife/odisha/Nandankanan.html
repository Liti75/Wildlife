Nandankanan Zoological Park

Location & Area: Near Bhubaneswar (~15 km). Established in 1960 and opened in 1963; India’s first zoo to join WAZA in 2009 :contentReference[oaicite:17]{index=17}.

Geography & Habitat

Set inside a natural forest adjoining Chandaka‑Dampara Wildlife Sanctuary, around Kanjia lake; mix of cages & open-moat enclosures :contentReference[oaicite:18]{index=18}.

Flora & Fauna

  • Over 156 species: 41 mammals, 83 birds, 26 reptiles, 6 amphibians; conservation‐breeding of white tigers, gharials, Indian pangolins; second-largest heronry of open‑billed storks in Odisha :contentReference[oaicite:19]{index=19}.

Activities

  • White tiger safari, lion enclosure drive, herpetarium, bird aviary, zoo trails and educational exhibits :contentReference[oaicite:20]{index=20}.

Accessibility & Permits

15 km from Bhubaneswar via road; publicly accessible daily; booking for safari sections and special enclosures required.

Significance

Pioneering zoo for species conservation in India; integrated ex‑situ and in‑situ reserve with adjoining forest sanctuary.
