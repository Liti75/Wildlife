Kuldiha Wildlife Sanctuary

Location & Area: Located in Balasore District, Odisha; spans ~272.75 km² across the Eastern Ghats. Declared on 4 January 1984; part of the Mayurbhanj (Tenda) Elephant Reserve and Similipal–Kuldiha–Hadgarh corridor. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

Geography & Habitat

Situated in the Chota Nagpur Plateau region, the sanctuary features moist mixed deciduous and peninsular Sal forest drained by the Kuldiha stream network. It's ecologically linked to Simlipal National Park via the Sukhupada and Nato hills. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

Flora & Fauna

  • Flora: Dominant species include Sal (Shorea robusta), Jamun, Piyasal, Bahera, Mango, Simul; orchids and moist forest understorey are well represented. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}
  • Fauna: Hosts Asian elephant, leopard, gaur, sloth bear, pangolin, mouse deer, giant squirrel, langur, ratel; bird species include hill myna, peafowl, hornbills and over 133 species recorded. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

Activities

  • Community-based eco‑tourism with jungle safari, hikes, bird watching, and stays in tented camps or forest rest houses around Rissia, Gohirabola, and Jhorachua. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

Accessibility & Permits

Approximately 27 km from Balasore and ~180 km from Bhubaneswar. Entry regulated by Odisha Forest Dept.; advance booking required for Eco‑tourism camps. Sanctuary closes for day visitors during monsoon (Jul–Sep), but night‑stay facilities remain operational. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}

Significance

Integral part of Simlipal‑Hadgarh elephant corridor; crucial for elephant movement and elephant population genetics in eastern India; joins national tiger and elephant conservation landscapes. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}
