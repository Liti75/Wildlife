Kotgarh Wildlife Sanctuary

Location & Area: Kandhamal (Baliguda subdivision), Odisha; spans ~399 km². Declared on 3 December 1981; IUCN Category IV. Linked with elephant corridor landscape. :contentReference[oaicite:18]{index=18}

Geography & Habitat

Moist deciduous forests with grassy meadows, nestled among Eastern Ghats foothills near Daringbadi; includes deep oak-sal forest and stream valleys. :contentReference[oaicite:19]{index=19}

Flora & Fauna

  • Flora: Rich botanical diversity (~650 species) including Sal, Sisoo, Kendu, Kusum, Mahua, Teak, bamboo. :contentReference[oaicite:20]{index=20}
  • Fauna: Tigers, Asian elephants, gaur, sambar, spotted deer, chousingha, jackal, sloth bear, pangolin, peafowl, red junglefowl, reptiles. :contentReference[oaicite:21]{index=21}

Activities

  • Wildlife spotting, eco-trekking, scenic forest trails, visits to tribal hamlets and streams in Daringbadi region. :contentReference[oaicite:22]{index=22}

Accessibility & Permits

~140 km from Phulbani; nearest town Baliguda. Access by road; permit and guide required, especially for deeper forest zones. :contentReference[oaicite:23]{index=23}

Significance

Major biodiversity zone in southern Odisha; part of proposed elephant corridors; supports tribal habitats and endemic wildlife. :contentReference[oaicite:24]{index=24}
