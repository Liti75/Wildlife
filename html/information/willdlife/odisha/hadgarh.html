Hadgarh Wildlife Sanctuary

Location & Area: Keonjhar District, Odisha; ~191 km²; created 6 December 1978 in Salandi River catchment area :contentReference[oaicite:12]{index=12}.

Geography & Habitat

On Chota Nagpur Plateau with mixed deciduous Sal forests, stream-fed grasslands and the Salandi Dam reservoir system :contentReference[oaicite:13]{index=13}.

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Tigers, leopards, pangolins, wolves, hyenas, porcupine, fishing/jungle cats, langurs, mugger crocodiles in reservoir, numerous birds (>150 species) :contentReference[oaicite:14]{index=14}.
  • Flora: Sal, Neem, Acacia, Arjun, Casuarina along with grasses and riparian shrubs along river and dam areas :contentReference[oaicite:15]{index=15}.

Activities

  • Nature walks, birdwatching around reservoir, limited guided trails and scenic picnicking near Salandi dam :contentReference[oaicite:16]{index=16}.

Accessibility & Permits

Approximately 122 km from Keonjhar (~250 km from Bhubaneswar). Accessible via Anandapur → Salandi Dam. Entry regulated by Forest Dept, best visited Oct–Mar :contentReference[oaicite:17]{index=17}.

Significance

Part of Similipal–Hadgarh–Kuldiha corridor, key elephant and carnivore reserve, also significant for crocodile and avifaunal habitats :contentReference[oaicite:18]{index=18}.
