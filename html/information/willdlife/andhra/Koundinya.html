Koundinya Wildlife Sanctuary

Location & Area: In Chittoor district, covers ~357.6 km²; declared in December 1990. It is uniquely home to Asian elephants in Andhra Pradesh.

Geography & Habitat

Rugged terrain with hills and valleys near Karnataka–Tamil Nadu border; covered with southern tropical dry deciduous forests and thorn scrub. Includes Kaigal and Kalyana Revu waterfalls.

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Asian elephants (~70 +), sloth bear, panther, cheetal, sambar, jackal, porcupine, slender loris, starred tortoise, yellow-throated bulbul, jungle cat.
  • Flora: Species such as Albizia amara, Acacia, Ficus, bamboo, Lagerstroemia, Santalum album regeneration.

Activities

  • Elephant safaris and wildlife tracking
  • Nature photography and bird watching
  • Waterfalls treks (Kaigal & Kalyana Revu)

Accessibility & Permits

Accessible by road from Chittoor and Bangalore; entry fees apply; permit required for photography and research.

Significance

The only elephant-inhabited sanctuary in Andhra Pradesh; key site for Project Elephant corridor planning.
