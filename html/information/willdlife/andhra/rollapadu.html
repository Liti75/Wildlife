Rollapadu Wildlife Sanctuary

Location & Area: Nandyal District, Andhra Pradesh; ~6.14 km²; notified April 1988 to protect Great Indian Bustard.

Geography & Habitat

Undulating plains, open grasslands with thorny scrub between the Nallamalai and Yerramalai ranges. Climate is hot, arid, with sparse rainfall.

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Great Indian Bustard, Lesser Florican, blackbuck, Indian wolf, jackal, bonnet macaque, Indian fox, over 130 bird species, reptiles like monitor lizards and Russell’s viper.
  • Flora: Grass species such as Chrysopogon, Heteropogon, Aristida along with Cassia fistula, Butea monosperma, Acacia spp.

Activities

  • Bird and wildlife observation (GIB focus), eco‑trails in dry grasslands
  • Photography and field studies with local guides (permit only)

Accessibility & Permits

About 40 km from Nandyal; permits issued by Andhra Pradesh Forest Department. No tourism infrastructure.

Significance

State’s only sanctuary for the Great Indian Bustard; critical grassland ecosystem under threat from habitat changes.
