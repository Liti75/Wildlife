Kambalakonda Wildlife Sanctuary

Location & Area: Adjacent to Visakhapatnam city; area ~70.70 km²; declared 10 Mar 1970 (sanctuary status 2002). Dry evergreen mixed with scrub & meadows.

Geography & Habitat

Hilly terrain with steep slopes, featuring Eastern Ghats dry evergreen forest interspersed with scrub, meadows and a reservoir.

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Indian leopard, spotted deer, barking deer, Indian pangolin, jackal, snakes (Russell's viper), birds including golden oriole, peafowl.
  • Flora: Dry evergreen trees, scrubland species and wild flowers like jungle berry, bush plum, screw tree.

Activities

  • Trekking (3 km & 7 km trails), boating, zip‑line, Burma bridge and eco‑park.
  • Wildlife spotting, birdwatching, camera‑trap biodiversity studies.

Accessibility & Permits

Just 3 km from Visakhapatnam city; entry fee charged, eco‑tourism zone managed under APFD. Permits required for research.

Significance

A major urban green lung and eastern Ghats biodiversity node, also serving as a city‑based sanctuary with tourism and conservation programming.
