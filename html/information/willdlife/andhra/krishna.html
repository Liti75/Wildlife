Krishna Wildlife Sanctuary

Location & Area: Near Machilipatnam, Andhra Pradesh; covers ~194.81 km²; IUCN Category IV estuarine protected area.

Geography & Habitat

Estuarine sanctuary supporting dense primary mangrove forests along the Krishna River delta, one of South India's last intact mangrove tracts.

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Mangrove-dependent birds, fish nurseries, crustaceans; estuarine ecology supports rich biodiversity.
  • Flora: Thick primary mangrove forests—rare in South India—under threat from habitat loss.

Activities

  • Birdwatching, mangrove nature walks
  • Fisheries ecology and wetland studies (research only)

Accessibility & Permits

Access managed by Andhra Pradesh Forest Department; permit required for scientific visits.

Significance

A critical mangrove sanctuary in South India, important for coastal stabilization, fisheries, bird breeding, and carbon sequestration.
