Coringa Wildlife Sanctuary

Location & Area: Near Kakinada, Andhra Pradesh; covers 235.7 km². Established on 5 July 1978. Governing body: Andhra Pradesh Forest Department.

Geography & Habitat

Situated in the Godavari estuary, this sanctuary features extensive mangrove forests and delta wetlands intersected by rivers like Gouthami and Coringa. Approximately half the area comprises backwaters with an 18 km sandpit for turtle nesting.

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Fishing cat, smooth‑coated otter, golden jackal, white‑backed and long‑billed vultures, olive ridley turtles nesting Jan–Mar; over 120 bird species including egret, kingfisher, painted stork.
  • Flora: 24 mangrove tree species including Avicennia, Rhizophora, Sonneratia; also marsh herbs like Suaeda and Salicornia.

Activities

  • Boat safaris and mangrove boardwalk (2‑km path)
  • Birdwatching and turtle nesting observation
  • Eco‑tour and nature trails via the interpretation centre

Accessibility & Permits

Located ~18 km from Kakinada; accessible by road followed by boat through mangroves. Permission required from Andhra Pradesh Forest Department for research or guided visits.

Significance

The third-largest mangrove ecosystem in India, crucial for coastal protection, biodiversity, and community livelihoods. The estuary supports fisheries and study of climate‑resilience.
