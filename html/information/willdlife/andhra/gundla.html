Gundla Brahmeswaram Wildlife Sanctuary

Location & Area: In Nandyal district's Nallamala hills; covers approx. 1194 km²; established 18 Sept 1990.

Geography & Habitat

Part of Nagarjunsagar–Srisailam Tiger Reserve; terrain features plateaus, ridges, gorges, valleys, and the Gundlakamma River.

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Tigers, panthers, wild dog, langurs, pangolin, sambar, nilgai, ratel, bats, lesser horseshoe bat and rusty-spotted cat.
  • Flora: Over 353 plant species, including endemic and critically endangered medicinal plants. Forest types: mixed deciduous, semi-evergreen, scrub, savannah.

Activities

  • Wildlife tracking and tiger monitoring
  • Butterfly surveys (notably Blue tiger species)
  • Botanical and ecological research

Accessibility & Permits

Access via Andhra Pradesh Forest Department permits; regulated entry due to reserve status.

Significance

Core area of Nallamala tiger reserve, vital for flagship species and plant biodiversity in southern India.
