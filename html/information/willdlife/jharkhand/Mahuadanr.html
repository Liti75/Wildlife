Mahuadanr Wolf Sanctuary

Location & Area: Latehar District, Jharkhand; area ~63.25 km². Established 1976, part of Palamau Tiger Reserve. :contentReference[oaicite:16]{index=16}

Geography & Habitat

Dry deciduous forest in Palamau plateau landscape, terrain of rolling hills and open woodlands. :contentReference[oaicite:17]{index=17}

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Indian wolf (primary protection), hyena, spotted deer, wild boar, bear; estimated 70 wolves in 2024 across four packs. :contentReference[oaicite:18]{index=18}
  • Flora: Typical deciduous woodland species supporting mammalian prey base.

Activities

  • Scientific wolf surveys, corridor conservation, limited regulated wildlife viewing (permit only).

Accessibility & Permits

Entry via Palamau Tiger Reserve; forest department authorization required. Limited public tourism due conservation mandate. :contentReference[oaicite:19]{index=19}

Significance

India’s only wolf sanctuary, critical for Indian grey wolf conservation in central plateau landscape. :contentReference[oaicite:20]{index=20}
