Udhwa Lake Bird Sanctuary

Location & Area: Sahibganj District, Jharkhand; ~5.65 km² (565 ha). Jharkhand’s only bird sanctuary. Ramsar‑designated 1 Feb 2025. :contentReference[oaicite:22]{index=22}

Geography & Habitat

Two connected backwater lakes—Pataura (~155 ha) and Berhale (~410 ha)—linked via channel to River Ganga, set amidst Rajmahal hills. :contentReference[oaicite:23]{index=23}

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: ~83 bird species recorded including endangered band‑tailed fish eagle, lesser adjutant, Pallas’s fish eagle, oriental darter, vulture species. ~18,000 birds counted in January 2024 census. :contentReference[oaicite:24]{index=24}
  • Flora: Aquatic vegetation, sedges like *Cyperus tagetum*, marsh grasses; approx. 30 aquatic plant species. :contentReference[oaicite:25]{index=25}

Activities

  • Winter birdwatching, boat rides on backwater lakes, nature photography; best from October–May. :contentReference[oaicite:26]{index=26}

Accessibility & Permits

11 km southeast of Rajmahal; nearest rail: Sahibganj Junction (~10 km). Entry regulated by forest authority; minimal fees. :contentReference[oaicite:27]{index=27}

Significance

Jharkhand’s only bird sanctuary and first Ramsar wetland. Critical habitat for migratory and threatened bird species. Recognized as Important Bird Area. :contentReference[oaicite:28]{index=28}
