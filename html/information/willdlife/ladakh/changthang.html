Changthang Wildlife Sanctuary

Location & Area: Leh district, Ladakh (J&K until 2019); ~4,000 km² on the Ladakhi Changthang plateau; declared 1987. Sandwiched between Tso Moriri, Tso Kar & Pangong Tso lake systems. ([turn0search14]turn0search8)

Geography & Habitat

Cold desert at 4,250–5,800 m altitude; vast grasslands, saline wetlands (Tso Kar, Tso Moriri), high‑altitude desert, and Indus River valleys. Sparse vegetation dominated by Stipa and Kobresia grasses and cushion herbs. ([turn0search3]turn0search1)

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Kiang (Tibetan wild ass), Tibetan antelope (chiru), Tibetan gazelle, bharal (blue sheep), wild yak, snow leopard, Tibetan wolf, Eurasian lynx, Himalayan marmot, pika; ~44 migratory waterbird species and black-necked crane. ([turn0search6]turn0search3turn0search16turn0search18)
  • Flora: Cold desert grasses (Stipa, Kobresia), cushion plants (Thylacospermum, Androsace), medicinal herbs such as Arnebia euchroma, Rheum spiciforme, Nepeta, Ephedra. ([turn0search1]turn0search0turn0search14)

Activities

  • High-altitude trekking around Korzok and Tso Moriri, birdwatching (cranes & geese), astro‑tourism at Hanle (India’s first Dark Sky Reserve). ([turn0search10]turn0reddit20turn0search8)

Conservation & Threats

MoEFCC‐designated eco-fragile zone; conservation challenges include tourism pressure, livestock grazing, climate change, and stray dog predation impacting cranes and wildlife. ([turn0search0]turn0search11)

Accessibility & Permits

~125 km from Leh via Man Leh highway. District Magistrate permit needed; restricted access for foreigners; ranger permission via Ladakh UT wildlife department. ([turn0search9]turn0search8)

Significance

Among India’s largest high-altitude sanctuaries and major breeding grounds for endangered plateau wildlife. Hanle within the area is key for star-gazing tourism and astronomical observatories. ([turn0search18]turn0search10)
