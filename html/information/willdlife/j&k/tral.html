Tral Wildlife Sanctuary

Location & Area: Pulwama district, ~44 km from Srinagar; established 26 October 2019 to protect the critically endangered Hangul deer; ~unknown area by merging Panyar‑Shikargah and Khiram reserves (~14 Hangul as of 2024) :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

Geography & Habitat

Moist temperate to alpine forest along Dachigam‑Overa corridor; includes sub‑alpine and alpine zones with oak, conifers, fspring‑fed meadows :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Hangul (Kashmir stag), musk deer, Kashmir gray langur, leopard, and ~200 bird species :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}
  • Flora: Aesculus indica, Fraxinus hookeri, Juglans regia, Parrotiopsis jacquemontiana, Viburnum, Jasminum spp. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

Activities

  • Wildlife trekking, Hangul spotting, birdwatching in ecologically sensitive habitats

Accessibility & Permits

~44 km from Srinagar; entry regulated via forest department; ideal trek with permits :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}
