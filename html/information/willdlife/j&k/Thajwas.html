Thajwas (Baltal‑Thajwas) Wildlife Sanctuary

Location & Area: Ganderbal district, near Sonamarg, J&K; spans ~203 km². Notified 19 March 1987; officially managed from 1990. Main habitat for Kashmir Musk Deer and Himalayan brown bear. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

Geography & Habitat

High-altitude sanctuary in the Zanskar range (3,015–5,466 m), glacier-fed valleys including Thajwas glacier and Sindh river catchment. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Kashmir musk deer, Himalayan brown and black bears, snow leopard, Himalayan ibex, Himalayan monal, snowcock, vulture species (~120+ birds). :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}
  • Flora: Alpine meadows, coniferous & subalpine forests, shrubs and highland herbaceous vegetation. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

Activities

  • Glacier trekking (Sonamarg–Thajwas), wildlife observation, high-altitude birdwatching, eco-sensitive tourism protocols. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

Accessibility & Permits

Approximately 76 km east of Srinagar via Sonamarg. Permits required from J&K Wildlife Dept; vehicular access banned within sanctuary to protect fragility. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}

Significance

High-altitude ecological corridor linking Dachigam and Overa‑Aru sanctuaries. Recently designated eco-fragile zone by MoEFCC to regulate tourism and development. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}
