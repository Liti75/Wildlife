Surinsar–Mansar Lakes Wildlife Sanctuary

Location & Area: Covers ~3.5 km² around twin lakes in Samba/Udhampur/Jammu districts; established 2005; Ramsar Wetland since Nov 2005 :contentReference[oaicite:14]{index=14}

Geography & Habitat

Wetland ecosystem perched on Shivalik hills; surrounded by northern dry mixed deciduous and Chir pine forests; part of Tawi river catchment :contentReference[oaicite:15]{index=15}

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Nilgai, leopard, barking deer, wild boar, fox, jackal, cheetal; rich aquatic birdlife including tufted duck, moorhen, grebe; red‑listed turtles and endemic Mansariella lacustris :contentReference[oaicite:16]{index=16}
  • Flora: Pinus roxburghii, Acacia catechu, Dalbergia sissoo, Ziziphus jujuba, Dodonaea scrub, Mallotus philippensis :contentReference[oaicite:17]{index=17}

Activities

  • Boat rides, birdwatching, temple visits and nature walks around lake periphery :contentReference[oaicite:18]{index=18}

Accessibility & Permits

~58 km from Jammu; nearest rail/air: Jammu; permits via forest/wildlife dept; regulated eco‑tour development under NPCA plans underway (2025–26) :contentReference[oaicite:19]{index=19}
