Tata Kutti Wildlife Sanctuary

Location & Area: Surankote Tehsil, Poonch District; covers ~66.27 km² (6,627 ha), adjacent to Hirpora Sanctuary. Created to protect markhor and musk deer habitats. ([turn0search8]turn0search14turn0search2)

Geography & Habitat

Eastern Pir Panjal range along Mughal Road corridor (altitude ~1,700–4,700 m). Ecosystems range from subalpine meadows to high-altitude fir-conifer forest and alpine pastures. ([turn0search2]turn0search0)

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Pir Panjal Markhor (Capra falconeri), musk deer, Himalayan black bear, leopard, Himalayan langur, ibex, snow leopard; Western tragopan captured on camera in 2024. (~16 mammal species and 112+ bird species). ([turn0search0]turn0search6turn0search2)
  • Flora: Moist kail and mixed coniferous forests interspersed with oak, horse-chestnut, walnut, birch; alpine meadows rich in medicinal herbs like Podophyllum, Aconitum, Bergenia, Betula utilis. ([turn0search2])

Activities

  • Wildlife observation, birdwatching (especially markhor and tragopan), ecological treks, conservation research treks along Mughal Road. ([turn0search2]turn0search6)

Accessibility & Permits

Located ~82 km from Poonch District HQ via Mughal Road. Permits required from J&K Wildlife Dept; eco-sensitive zone notified. Case-based releases for rescued wildlife occur here (e.g. black bear release 2024). ([turn0search2]turn0search18)

Significance

A core refuge for the endangered markhor goat and linked to Hirpora corridor, supporting high-altitude Himalayan biodiversity and migratory pastoral systems. ([turn0search0]turn0search2turn0search6)
