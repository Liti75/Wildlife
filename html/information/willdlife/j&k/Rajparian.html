Rajparian (Daksum) Wildlife Sanctuary

Location & Area: Anantnag district, ~42 km from Anantnag city (~85 km south of Srinagar); area ~49 km²; upgraded to sanctuary in 1981 from former hangul reserve :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}

Geography & Habitat

Sub-alpine pastures and coniferous forest (deodar, pine) in Pir Panjal range, with riverine valleys and wetlands :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Hangul (Kashmir stag), musk deer, Himalayan serow, Himalayan marmot, flying squirrel, yellow‑throated marten, rich birdlife including Himalayan pheasants and raptors :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}
  • Flora: Mountain conifer forest with dense deodar, pine, spruce, and alpine grasses in meadows.

Activities

  • Pilgrimage and trekking routes to Daksum hill; wildlife and birdwatching amid serene forest meadows.

Accessibility & Permits

Nearest rail: Anantnag (~42 km); forest permits required via local forest office :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}

Significance

Critical hangul habitat within Dachigam‑Overa corridor; remnant high-altitude biodiversity hotspot.
