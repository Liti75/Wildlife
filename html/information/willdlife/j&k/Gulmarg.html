Gulmarg Wildlife Sanctuary

Location & Area: Baramulla district near Gulmarg hill station (~50 km SW of Srinagar); ~180 km²; designated a sanctuary in 1987 (previously game reserve since 1981) :contentReference[oaicite:11]{index=11}

Geography & Habitat

Temperate to alpine terrain, elevation 2,400–4,300 m; sub-alpine forests, meadows, rocky cliffs in Pir Panjal range :contentReference[oaicite:12]{index=12}

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Musk deer, Himalayan black bear, common leopard, barking deer, snow cock, Indian wolf, snow leopard occasionally; bird species include Kashmir flycatcher and alpine pheasants :contentReference[oaicite:13]{index=13}
  • Flora: Blue pine, silver birch, silver fir, yew, maple in forest zones; alpine grasses and wildflowers higher up :contentReference[oaicite:14]{index=14}

Activities

  • Trekking, gondola rides up to Apharwat peak, alpine meadow walks, wildlife and birdwatching.

Accessibility & Permits

~48 km from Srinagar; nearest rail Baramulla (~26 km); permits via J&K Wildlife Dept needed for protected zones :contentReference[oaicite:15]{index=15}

Significance

Major Himalayan sanctuary combining scenic high-altitude biodiversity, important musk deer and alpine bird habitat.
