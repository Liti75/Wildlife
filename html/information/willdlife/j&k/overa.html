Overa‑Aru Wildlife Sanctuary

Location & Area: Anantnag district, near Pahalgam, J&K; ~511 km²; declared a sanctuary in 1981 from earlier game reserve established 1945 under Dogra rule :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

Geography & Habitat

Located in Lidder Valley, bordered north by Sind Valley, west by Dachigam NP; elevation up to Kolahoi peak (~5,425 m) and alpine lakes (e.g. Tarsar) :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Kashmir stag (Hangul), musk deer, brown bear, snow leopard, Himalayan black bear, leopard, langur, red fox, over 120 bird species including Egyptian vulture, Kashmir flycatcher, warblers :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}
  • Flora: Silver fir, blue pine, oak, birch, alpine shrubs and meadows in glacial catchments :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

Activities

  • Trekking to Tarsar–Marsar lakes, wildlife viewing and bird‑watching; scenic Kashmir Valley vistas.

Accessibility & Permits

~76 km east of Srinagar; nearest rail/airport Srinagar; forest department permits required for entry and trekking :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

Significance

Important high-altitude ecological corridor and IBA supporting Hangul conservation; catchment for Lidder river feeding agricultural regions :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}
