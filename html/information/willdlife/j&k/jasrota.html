Jasrota Wildlife Sanctuary

Location & Area: Kathua District, Jammu & Kashmir, on the banks of Ujh River near Jasrota Village; covers ~10.04 km². Notified March 1987 under J&K Wildlife Act. ([turn0search3]turn0search19)

Geography & Habitat

Low-elevation (~336–520 m) Shivalik foothill terrain with bamboo-dominated mixed dry deciduous forest along the Ujh and its tributaries. ([turn0search19]turn0search3)

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Axis deer (cheetal), barking deer, sambar, wild boar, rhesus macaque, jackal, peafowl, red junglefowl, green pigeon, blue rock pigeon, waterfowl like teal and pochard. ([turn0search5]turn0search19turn0search15)
  • Flora: Dominated by bamboo, Sissoo, Acacia catechu, Lannea grandis, Dodonaea viscosa, Lantana camara and scrub vegetation. ([turn0search1]turn0search19)

Activities

  • Birdwatching (Mar–May), mammal spotting (Sep–Mar), light trekking near historical Jasrota Fort and Ujh Barrage. ([turn0search5]turn0search19turn0search15)

Accessibility & Permits

Located ~63–75 km from Jammu; nearest rail: Kathua (~12–25 km); airport: Jammu. Entry and vehicle permits via regional Wildlife Warden. ([turn0search19]turn0search3turn0search11)

Significance

Small but ecologically and culturally important sanctuary preserving low-land riparian forest and birdlife near historic Jasrota Fort and Ujh wetlands. ([turn0search7]turn0search9turn0search3)
