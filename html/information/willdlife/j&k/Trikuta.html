Trikuta Wildlife Sanctuary (De‑notified)

Status: Originally declared wildlife sanctuary in 1981 (~31.8 km² area) around Vaishno Devi hills. De‑notified by Supreme Court order in 2013; no longer a protected area. :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}

Historical Geography & Habitat

Located near Katra town in Reasi district; comprised Shivalik foothills around Trikuta peaks with mixed deciduous forest and scrubland. :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}/p>

Fauna Mentioned in Documents

Historically reported presence of leopards, cheer pheasants, goral and small herbivores prior to de‑notification. Detailed wildlife data limited. :contentReference[oaicite:11]{index=11}/p>

Legal & Conservation Notes

Sanctuary ceased to exist following apex court’s de‑notification, which allowed land use for shrine expansion and mining. Replacement protected areas were advised. :contentReference[oaicite:12]{index=12}/p>

Significance

Served to illustrate challenges in balancing conservation and development in high‑pressure pilgrimage zones; affiliated with proposals to establish replacement protected sites. :contentReference[oaicite:13]{index=13}/p>
