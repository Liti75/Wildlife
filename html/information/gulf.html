Gulf of Mannar Biosphere Reserve

Location & Area: Situated along the Tamil Nadu coast, covering ~10,500 km² including 21 islands and rich marine ecosystems.

Geography & Habitat

The reserve lies between India and Sri Lanka, featuring coral reefs, seagrass beds, mangroves, and lagoons, supporting exceptional marine biodiversity.

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Dugongs (sea cows), dolphins, sea turtles, reef fish, and over 3600 marine species.
  • Flora: Seagrass species, mangroves, and algae forming vital marine habitats.

Activities

  • Marine eco-tourism and snorkeling
  • Research on coral reefs and seagrass ecosystems
  • Birdwatching in coastal habitats

Accessibility & Permits

Nearest city: Rameswaram. Accessible by road and rail from Madurai and Chennai. Special permits are required for island visits.

Significance

India’s first marine biosphere reserve, recognized for protecting endangered species like dugongs and conserving fragile coral ecosystems.
