Jaldapara National Park

Location & Area: Situated in Alipurduar District, northern West Bengal at the foothills of the Eastern Himalayas along the Torsa River; area ~216.5 km². Declared a sanctuary in 1941 and upgraded to National Park status in 2014

Geography & Habitat

A mosaic of open savannah grasslands interspersed with riverine and mixed deciduous forests, intersected by rivers such as the Torsa, Malangi, Hollong, Chirakhawa and Sissamara. Elevation around 61 m above sea level

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Largest population of Indian one‑horned rhinoceros in West Bengal (second in India after Kaziranga); also hosts Asian elephants, gaur (Indian bison), Royal Bengal tigers, leopards, sambar, chital, barking deer, hog deer, wild pig and Hispid hare
  • Birdlife: Nearly 240 species including Bengal florican, crested eagles, Pallas’s fish eagle, shikra, Finn’s weaver, Indian hornbill, peafowl, partridges, herons, wagtails and waterfowl
  • Flora: Dominated by tall elephant grasses (Saccharum spp., Themeda, Setaria palmifolia, Phragmites, Imperata), interspersed with riverine trees such as sal, sissoo, khair, simul and siris

Activities & Amenities

  • Early‑morning elephant safaris (especially in the eastern and Chilapata ranges) offer close rhino encounters
  • Jeep safaris through tall grass corridors and forest edge zones
  • Birdwatching drives and guided nature walks; interpretation centers at Hollong area and Madarihat

Accessibility & Best Time

Nearest airport: Bagdogra (~125 km); nearest railway stations: Madarihat / New Alipurduar (~7 km). Accessible via NH‑317 through Alipurduar–Madarihat. Ideal visiting period: November to March; avoid monsoon months (June–September)
