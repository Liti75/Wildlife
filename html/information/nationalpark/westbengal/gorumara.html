Gorumara National Park

Location & Area: Located in Jalpaiguri district, West Bengal, in the Eastern Himalayan foothills; area ~80 km²; declared a National Park in 1992.

Geography & Habitat

Situated on the floodplains of the Murti, Raidak, and Jaldhaka Rivers. The park features riverine grasslands and sal-dominated moist deciduous forests.

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Indian rhinoceros, Indian elephant, gaur, sloth bear, sambar, chital, barking deer, and various reptiles and amphibians.
  • Birdlife: Includes scarlet minivet, Indian hornbill, peacocks, woodpeckers, flycatchers, and migratory species like Brahminy duck.
  • Flora: Sal, teak, simul, siris, khair, bamboo, and tropical riverine vegetation.

Activities & Amenities

  • Watchtowers like Rhino Point, Jatraprasad, and Chukchuki for wildlife and birdwatching.
  • Jeep safaris and village tours with cultural dance performances.
  • Nature interpretation center at Lataguri.

Accessibility & Best Time

Nearest airport is Bagdogra (~80 km), rail stations include New Mal Junction and Chalsa. Best time to visit is November to March. Closed during monsoons (June to September).
