Singalila National Park

Location & Area: Darjeeling district, West Bengal, India; situated along the Singalila Ridge at elevations from ~2,300 m to ~3,636 m. Area ~78.6 km²; notified sanctuary in 1986 and national park in 1992

Geography & Habitat

The park spans alpine meadows, temperate rhododendron-oak forests, and subtropical pine stands across steep Himalayan slopes. Sandakphu (3,636 m) and Phalut (3,595 m) are the highest points offering panoramic views of Everest, Kangchenjunga, Lhotse, and Makalu

Flora & Fauna

  • Flora: Rich in 18 rhododendron species, over 600 orchids, magnolias, primulas, arisaema (Cobra Lily), primulas and bamboo undergrowth between 1,800–4,000 m
  • Mammals: Red panda (reintroduced), clouded leopard, Himalayan black bear, yellow-throated marten, barking deer, leopard cat, pangolin, takin and serow
  • Birdlife: Over 120 species including blood pheasant, satyr tragopan, kalij pheasant, fire-tailed myzornis, golden‑breasted fulvetta, scarlet minivet, and various laughingthrushes and parrotbills

Activities & Routes

  • Trek the classic Manebhanjan–Sandakphu–Phalut route: multi-day trek through scenic villages and alpine meadows
  • Jeep safari using vintage Land Rovers available from Manebhanjan to Sandakphu/Phalut (~2.5–5.5 h drives)
  • Birdwatching, flower photography during rhododendron bloom (Mar–May), and wildlife spotting incl. red panda & pheasants

Accessibility & Best Time

Nearest airport: Bagdogra (~90–100 km). Rail-heads: New Jalpaiguri (broad gauge) and Ghoom (narrow gauge) ~30 km from park entry. Access via NH‑31A through Darjeeling to Manebhanjan, entry open typically 7 AM–2 PM (vehicle limit enforced) Park remains closed during monsoon (15 June–15 September). Best months: Spring (March–May) and post‑monsoon/autumn (mid‑Sep to Dec) when blooming and wildlife activity is peak Entry fee for Indian nationals ~₹100; foreigners ₹200; camera permit ~₹100, video ₹400; guides required for trekking, optional for jeep safari
