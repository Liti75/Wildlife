Sundarbans Biosphere Reserve

Location & Area: Spanning parts of West Bengal, India; area ~9,630 km², including mangrove forests, mudflats, and tidal waterways.

Geography & Habitat

The Sundarbans comprise the world’s largest mangrove ecosystem, intersected by a network of rivers, estuaries, and tidal creeks. The region lies at the delta of the Ganges, Brahmaputra, and Meghna rivers.

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Famous for the Bengal tiger, estuarine crocodile, fishing cat, spotted deer, and a wide variety of migratory birds.
  • Flora: Predominantly mangrove species like Heritiera fomes (Sundari), Avicennia, and Nypa fruticans.

Activities

  • Boat safaris through mangrove channels
  • Birdwatching and wildlife photography
  • Visiting watchtowers at Sajnekhali, Dobanki, and Sudhanyakhali

Accessibility & Permits

Nearest town: Canning (48 km from Kolkata). Entry permits required for certain areas; boats can be hired from Godkhali, Namkhana, or Sajnekhali.

Significance

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Sundarbans play a critical role in coastal protection, climate regulation, and biodiversity conservation.
