Chandoli National Park

Location & Area: Sangli District, Western Maharashtra; area ~317 km² (part of Sahyadri Tiger Reserve)

Geography & Habitat

Situated in Sahyadri Range at 589–1,044 m elevation; rugged hills, lateritic plateaus (‘Saddas’), forest-dam-lake ecosystem.

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Bengal tigers, endemic birds, diverse wildlife of Western Ghats.
  • Flora: Moist deciduous forest, grasslands, freshwater bodies including Chandoli Dam and lakes.

Activities

  • Safari, trekking, boating, birdwatching

Accessibility & Permits

Access via Mandur village near Shirala taluk (~100 km from Sangli, ~78 km from Kolhapur).

Significance

Part of Sahyadri Tiger Reserve; also includes historical forts Prachitgad and Bhairavgad; declared UNESCO World Heritage site.
