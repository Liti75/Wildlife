Navegaon National Park

Location & Area: Gondia district, eastern Maharashtra; area ~135 km²; includes Dr Salim Ali Bird Sanctuary on Navegaon Lake

Geography & Habitat

Lush green hills surrounding Navegaon Lake; mixed dry to moist deciduous forests; includes aviary and deer‑park facilities

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Around 209 bird species (60% of state’s birds), migratory waterbirds in winters; mammals include tiger, panther, leopard, wolf, sloth‑bear, deer species
  • Flora: Mixture of dry mixed and moist deciduous forest types

Activities

  • Birdwatching from watch‑tower, boat rides on lake, safaris to see mammals

Accessibility & Permits

Approximately 150 km from Nagpur; can reach via Gondia district; tourism infrastructure includes park museum and interpretation centre

Significance

An important avifaunal sanctuary, also contributes to tiger landscape connectivity in Vidarbha region
