Pench National Park

Location & Area: Straddles Seoni & Chhindwara (MP) and Nagpur (Maharashtra); total area ~758 km² (park core ~299 km²)

Geography & Habitat

Located in southern Satpura Range at elevation 425–620 m; mixed teak forests and riverine ecosystems along Pench River

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Tigers, leopards, deer, wild dog, rich birdlife; famously inspired Rudyard Kipling’s *The Jungle Book*
  • Flora: Teak‑mixed forests typical of the Satpura landscape

Activities

  • Jeep safari, birdwatching, nature walks, Chilapata forest trails

Accessibility & Permits

Accessible via Seoni or Nagpur; park crossing two states; safari booking via forest department

Significance

Important tiger reserve under Project Tiger (since 1992); high biodiversity; tourism linked to cultural heritage and literary fame
