Bhagwan Mahaveer Sanctuary & Mollem National Park

Location & Area: Goa’s largest protected area (≈ 240 km²), includes Mollem National Park (≈ 107 km²). Located in Dharbandora taluk near Molem village, ~57 km east of Panaji.

History & Designation

Originally established as Mollem Game Sanctuary in 1969, it was later renamed Bhagwan Mahaveer Wildlife Sanctuary. In 1978, the central 107 km² core was designated Mollem National Park.

Geography & Habitat

Situated in the Western Ghats’ foothills bisected by NH‑4A and the Mormugao–Londa railway. Habitats include tropical evergreen, semi‑evergreen, and moist deciduous forests, with dense canopy limiting undergrowth.

Flora & Fauna

  • Flora: Home to 722 species of flowering plants (128 regionally endemic), notable trees include Terminalia, Lagerstroemia, Xylia, and Dalbergia.
  • Fauna: Leopards (including black panther), Bengal tiger, gaur, sambar, spotted deer, wild boar, sloth bear, pangolin, slender loris, Malabar giant squirrel, bonnet macaque, mouse deer.
  • Birds: Over 120 species, including Goa’s state bird Ruby-throated Yellow Bulbul, Malabar hornbills, paradise flycatcher, crested serpent eagle, fairy bluebird, emerald dove.
  • Reptiles & Butterflies: King cobra, Indian rock python, Russell’s viper, etc.; insects include crimson rose, blue Mormon, southern birdwing and several endemic butterflies.

Attractions & Activities

  • Dudhsagar Falls: Four-tiered waterfall (~310 m), one of India’s tallest, within the park on the Mandovi River.
  • Tambdi Falls, Devil’s Canyon viewpoint, and the 12th‑century Tambdi Surla Temple attributed to Kadambas.
  • Nature trails, trekking, guided jeep safaris, birdwatching and camping.

Visitor Information

Open daily from ~8:00 AM to 5:30 PM. Entry: Rs 20 (adult), Rs 10 (child); still camera Rs 30, video camera Rs 150 :contentReference[oaicite:12]{index=12}.

Stay options include Goa Tourism and Forest Department cottages/dorms in Mollem; area offers multiple licensed safari operators and trekking routes.

Conservation & Significance

The sanctuary is part of the Western Ghats UNESCO‑declared biodiversity hotspot and houses rare endemic flora and fauna. It has also faced threats from mining and infrastructure expansion, prompting no‑mining movements like “Save Mollem”.
