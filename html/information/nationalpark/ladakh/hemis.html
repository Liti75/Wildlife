Hemis National Park

Location & Area: Situated in Ladakh, India; it spans approximately 4,400 km², making it the largest national park in South Asia. Established in 1981.

Geography & Habitat

Located in the eastern Ladakh region of the Himalayas. It includes the Markha, Rumbak, and Sumdah valleys, with altitudes ranging from 3,000 m to over 6,000 m. The region features alpine shrublands and meadows.

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Home to the rare snow leopard, Tibetan wolf, Eurasian brown bear, red fox, and blue sheep (bharal). It has the highest density of snow leopards in any protected area globally.
  • Flora: Alpine vegetation like juniper, dry alpine herbs, and shrubs; due to its high-altitude terrain, vegetation is sparse but adapted to cold desert conditions.

Activities

  • Trekking through scenic valleys like Markha and Rumbak
  • Wildlife spotting, especially snow leopard safaris in winter
  • Photography and birdwatching (especially in summer)

Accessibility & Permits

The park is accessible from Leh (~40 km). Best time to visit is between May and September. Special permits may be required for foreigners due to proximity to international borders.

Significance

Hemis is the only national park in India north of the Himalayas. It is globally significant for snow leopard conservation and part of the trans-Himalayan biogeographic zone.
