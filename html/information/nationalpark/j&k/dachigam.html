Dachigam National Park

Location & Area: Located 22 km from Srinagar, Jammu & Kashmir; covers ~141 km². Established in 1981, originally protected since 1910.

Geography & Habitat

Encompasses steep mountains and river valleys in the Zabarwan Range; altitude varies from 1,650 m to 4,200 m, giving rise to diverse habitats from alpine pastures to meadows and rocky cliffs.

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Critically endangered Kashmir stag (Hangul), leopards, Himalayan black bear, musk deer, Himalayan serow, and Himalayan weasel.
  • Flora: Wild cherry, plum, walnut, poplar, birch, and oak; alpine vegetation at higher altitudes.

Activities

  • Trekking and wildlife viewing (restricted)
  • Nature photography, birdwatching

Accessibility & Permits

Accessible via Srinagar; permits required from J&K Wildlife Department. Certain areas (Upper Dachigam) restricted for ecological sensitivity.

Significance

Only home of the Hangul deer; crucial for preserving Kashmir’s Himalayan biodiversity.
