Kishtwar National Park

Location & Area: Located in the Kishtwar district of Jammu & Kashmir; spread over ~2,190.50 km². Established in 1981.

Geography & Habitat

Located in the western Himalayas with altitudes ranging from 1,700 m to 4,800 m. It features rugged terrain, alpine meadows, and steep valleys along the Chenab River basin.

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Snow leopards, Himalayan black bear, brown bear, musk deer, ibex, and markhor; over 50 species of birds.
  • Flora: Deodar, fir, pine, juniper, birch, and alpine scrub species.

Activities

  • Eco-tourism, trekking (permit-based)
  • Wildlife observation, birdwatching

Accessibility & Permits

Accessible via Kishtwar town; entry requires special permission from the J&K Forest Department due to ecological sensitivity.

Significance

Important high-altitude ecosystem in the Himalayas; home to endangered snow leopards and diverse flora.
