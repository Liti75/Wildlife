Kazinag National Park

Location & Area: Located in Baramulla district, Jammu & Kashmir near India–Pakistan border; spans ~180 km²; proposed in 1992, declared national park in 2000.

Geography & Habitat

Part of the northwestern Himalayan biogeographic zone. Terrain includes snow-clad peaks, streams, and alpine meadows. Elevation varies between 1,800 m to over 4,700 m.

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Markhor (mountain goat), musk deer, Himalayan brown and black bear, leopard, and variety of pheasants and Himalayan birds.
  • Flora: Fir, pine, birch, and diverse alpine flora.

Activities

  • Nature observation (eco-sensitive zone)
  • Research and conservation-focused visits

Accessibility & Permits

Reachable via Baramulla or Uri; permits are necessary and strictly regulated due to its border proximity.

Significance

Known for rare species like Markhor; key part of Kazinag Mountain landscape conservation.
