Papikonda National Park

Location & Area: Near Rajamahendravaram in the Papi Hills, Andhra Pradesh; area ≈ 1,012.9 km². Designated as wildlife sanctuary in 1978 and national park in 2008. It’s an Important Bird and Biodiversity Area.

Geography & Climate

The park spans east and west Godavari districts at elevations from ~20 m to higher hills, with annual rainfall about 1,168 mm. It’s bisected by the Godavari River.

Flora & Fauna

  • Vegetation: Moist & dry deciduous forests – species include Pterocarpus marsupium, Terminalia, Sterculia, Anogeissus.
  • Mammals: Bengal tiger, Indian leopard, sloth bear, spotted deer, gaur, and more.
  • Birds: Black-bellied tern, Pale‑capped pigeon, Yellow‑throated bulbul, hornbill species.
  • Reptiles: King cobra, golden gecko.

Activities

  • Bird‑watching & wildlife photography
  • Trekking in scenic Papi Hill ranges

Threats & Conservation

Challenges include poaching, forest fires, and land clearing. Ongoing habitat protection and biodiversity monitoring are in place.
