Saddle Peak National Park

Location & Area: Situated on North Andaman Island around Diglipur, covering approximately 32.54 km², established in 1979. It's centered around the 732 m Saddle Peak – the highest point in the archipelago.

Geography & Climate

Dense tropical and evergreen forests cover steep hills. The climate is tropical oceanic with temperatures from 20–30 °C. Rainy season is June–October.

Flora & Fauna

  • Flora: Tropical moist evergreen and deciduous forests with unique species like Scolopia pusilla, Cleistanthus robustus.
  • Fauna: Andaman wild pig, hill myna, imperial pigeon, water monitor, dolphins, whales, saltwater crocodile.

Activities

  • 8 km trek to summit with three scenic viewpoints and forest rest sheds.
  • Wildlife spotting, bird‑watching, photography, nature walks.

Access & Entry

Approximately 300 km from Port Blair (via Diglipur), base at Kalipur or Lamiya Bay. Entry fee: ₹25 for Indians, ₹50 for foreigners. Permits required from Forest Department.
