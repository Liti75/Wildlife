Mount Harriet (Mount Manipur) National Park

Location & Area: In Hope Town, Port Blair. Established 1979, spanning ~46.6 km², its peak (383 m) is the second‑highest in South Andaman :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}.

Geography & History

Originally a reserve forest, converted to a national park in 1979. Contains evergreen and semi-evergreen forests, streams, and ridges up to 481 m :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}.

Named after Harriet Tytler, wife of British officer Robert C. Tytler; recently renamed Mount Manipur National Park in tribute to freedom fighters :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}.

Flora & Fauna

  • Flora: Dense evergreen & semi-evergreen; 393 plant species (74 endemic) including Ceropygie andamanica :contentReference[oaicite:11]{index=11}.
  • Fauna: Andaman wild pigs, saltwater crocodiles, robber crabs; 90 bird species (11 endemic), 27 reptiles/amphibians (12 endemic), and 16 freshwater fish :contentReference[oaicite:12]{index=12}.

Activities

  • Trekking trails with panoramic views of Port Blair, Ross and Havelock Islands :contentReference[oaicite:13]{index=13}.
  • Bird-watching, photography, enjoying scenic forests and viewpoints

Access & Facilities

Located ~15 km by ferry + trek or ~50 km via road from Port Blair :contentReference[oaicite:14]{index=14}. Entry fee applies; forest guest house available.
