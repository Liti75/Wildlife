Middle Button Island National Park

Location & Area: Situated ~200 km northeast of Port Blair, covering approx. 64 km². Established in 1979 and forms part of the Rani Jhansi Marine National Park :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}.

Geography & Climate

Tropical oceanic climate with temperatures 20–30 °C; heavy rains June–October. Best months: December–April :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}.

Flora & Fauna

  • Moist deciduous forests: species include Calamus palustris, Dipterocarpus grandiflorus, Hopea odorata :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}.
  • Land animals: spotted deer, water monitor lizards; marine life: dugong, dolphins, sea turtles, blue whales, vibrant corals :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}.

Activities

  • Scuba diving & snorkeling through rich underwater ecosystems
  • Wildlife watching (spotted deer, marine creatures)

Access & Permits

Accessible by boat from Port Blair; entry permits required. No accommodation on-island; camping only with prior permission :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}.
