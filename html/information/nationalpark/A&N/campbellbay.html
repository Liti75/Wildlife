Campbell Bay National Park

Location & Area: Located on Great Nicobar Island (Andaman & Nicobar, India), covering ~426 km². Gazetted in 1992 and part of the Great Nicobar Biosphere Reserve.

Geography & Climate

The park occupies the southern tip of Great Nicobar, with terrain ranging from steep hills to coastal plains and coral reefs. Mount Thullier (732 m) is its highest peak. It has a humid tropical climate, with temperatures between 20‑31 °C and marked wet/dry seasons. Best visited between March and October.

Flora

  • Tropical evergreen forests with orchids (e.g., Coelogyne nitida), tree ferns, and mangroves (e.g., Rhizophora mucronata).
  • Prominent species include Andaman padauk, Dipterocarpus, and epiphytes.

Fauna

  • Mammals: Nicobar tree shrew, Nicobar long‑tailed macaque, crab‑eating macaque, dugong, wild boar, palm civet, fruit bats.
  • Reptiles: Saltwater crocodile, reticulated python, giant robber crab, turtles (leatherback, hawksbill, Olive Ridley, Malayan box).
  • Birds: Nicobar pigeon, Nicobar megapode, serpent eagle, white‑bellied sea eagle, herons, woodpeckers, swiftlets.
  • Marine life: Coral reefs, plentiful fish species; snorkeling/diving are common.

Activities

  • Trekking, bird‑watching, wildlife spotting
  • Snorkeling & scuba-diving around coral reefs
  • Nature walks and guided safaris

Accessibility & Permits

Access is limited: Fly via Port Blair (Pawan Hans) then boat/helicopter to Great Nicobar. Special permits are required.

Conservation & Significance

Integral to the Great Nicobar Biosphere Reserve. Conservation efforts include habitat monitoring and protections post‑2004 tsunami.

Quick Facts

  • Designation: IUCN Category II National Park
  • Established: 1992
  • Part of UNESCO Biosphere Reserve since 2013
  • Rich endemic biodiversity & secluded location make it a high-conservation-value site.
