South Button Island National Park

Location & Area: A small island (~5 km²), part of Rani Jhansi Marine National Park, located ~24 km southwest of Havelock Island.

Geography & Climate

Features coral-rich shallow reefs (as shallow as 1.8 m). Oceanic climate with average temperatures 20–30 °C. Best visited December–April.

Flora & Fauna

  • Flora: Coastal trees & shrubs – rattan palm, bamboo species, Calophyllum, Dipterocarpus varieties.
  • Fauna: Dugongs, turtles, dolphins, blue whales. Reef fish: snappers, barracuda, angelfish, butterflyfish, manta rays, devil rays & nudibranchs. White-bellied sea eagles & swiftlets nest here.

Activities

  • Snorkelling & scuba‑diving in high-visibility shallow reefs (as little as 6 ft deep).
  • Marine life observation including turtles and manta rays.

Access & Permits

Reachable only by boat from Port Blair or Havelock. Permits needed; no on-island accommodation.
