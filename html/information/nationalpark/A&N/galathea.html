Galathea National Park

Location & Area: Located on Great Nicobar Island (Andaman & Nicobar Islands, India), covering roughly 110 km². Gazetted in 1992 and forming part of the Great Nicobar Biosphere Reserve alongside Campbell Bay National Park.

Geography & Climate

The park lies on the southern tip of Great Nicobar, featuring lowland rainforests, mangroves, and pristine beaches. It experiences an equatorial tropical climate with heavy rainfall (3000–3800 mm/year) and no cold winters.

Flora

  • Dominated by tropical wet evergreen and mangrove forests.
  • Over 2200 plant species recorded, including more than 200 endemic ones with medicinal uses.

Fauna

  • Mammals & Reptiles: Giant robber crab, crab-eating macaque, saltwater crocodile, water monitor, python, civets, fruit bats.
  • Birds: Nicobar pigeon, Nicobar megapode, sea eagles and more.
  • Turtles: Leatherback sea turtles nest here February–December—the largest turtle in the world.

Activities

  • Wildlife spotting (terrestrial, avian, marine)
  • Snorkeling & scuba around coral reefs
  • Nature hikes along rainforest, river & coast trails

Accessibility & Permits

Reachable via Port Blair → Campbell Bay by ferry or helicopter, then local boat to Galathea. Very remote—special permits required. Nearest airport: Port Blair (~523 km away via sea). Best time to visit: October–April.

Conservation & Significance

Integral to the UNESCO-designated Great Nicobar Biosphere Reserve (2013). Protects rare endemics and leatherback turtle nesting sites. Recent development proposals (including a container port) pose potential risks.

Quick Facts

  • IUCN Category II National Park; established 1992
  • Area: ~110 km²
  • Biosphere Reserve core zone since 1989, UNESCO in 2013
