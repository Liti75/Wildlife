Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park

Location & Area: Located near Wandoor Village, South Andaman (~29 km from Port Blair), this national park spans approximately 281.5 km², consisting of 15–17 islands and surrounding marine areas.

Geography & Climate

The park includes the Labyrinth (Boat, Red Skin) and Twin Islands, with terrain ranging from fringing reefs and mangroves to sandy beaches. It has a tropical monsoon climate: best to visit from November to May, monsoon (June–August) closes access.

History & Protection

Established on 24 May 1983 under India’s Wildlife Protection Act for conserving marine biodiversity—especially coral reefs and nesting turtles. It falls under IUCN Category II and is managed by the Andaman & Nicobar Forest Department.

Flora & Fauna

  • Coral reefs: fringing reefs featuring acropora, porites, tubipora, fungia, etc.
  • Marine life: starfish, sharks, colourful reef fishes, molluscs, turtles, and salt-water crocodiles
  • Birds: white-bellied sea eagle, herons, terns, waders, swiftlets, Andaman teal

Activities

  • Snorkeling & scuba-diving (glass-bottom boats available)
  • Island camping, nature walks, bird-watching, and coral reef viewing

Accessibility & Fees

Entry point: Wandoor pier (~30 km from Port Blair by bus/taxi). Permits: ₹50 entry, ₹25 camera, boat fees (~₹300–650 to visit Jolly Buoy or Red Skin). Only select islands are open during tourist season.

Significance

A key marine conservation zone in India, protecting crucial habitats like coral reefs and nesting grounds for sea turtles. Its ecological significance has grown amid climate change and coastal development pressures.
