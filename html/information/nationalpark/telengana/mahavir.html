Mahavir Harina Vanasthali National Park

Location & Area: Situated in Vanasthalipuram, Hyderabad, Telangana; spans ~3,600 acres (14.6 km²). Declared national park in 1975.

Geography & Habitat

The park is characterized by arid scrub forests, grassy meadows, and is named after Lord Mahavir to mark his 2500th birth anniversary.

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Blackbucks (antelope), cheetal, wild boar, monitor lizards, civets, mongooses, and over 80 species of birds.
  • Flora: Dry deciduous species like neem, acacia, cassia, and other hardy scrub vegetation.

Activities

  • Wildlife viewing from watchtowers and safari vans
  • Birdwatching and photography
  • Nature education center for students

Accessibility & Permits

Located on the Hyderabad–Vijayawada highway. Entry with ticket; open on most days except Mondays.

Significance

Provides crucial green cover and wildlife habitat within Hyderabad’s metropolitan boundary; known for its large blackbuck population.
