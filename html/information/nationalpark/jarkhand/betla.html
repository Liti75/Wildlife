Betla National Park

Location & Area: Located in Latehar & Palamu districts, Jharkhand; covers approx. 226.32 km². Declared a tiger reserve in 1974 and a national park in 1986.

Geography & Habitat

Situated on the Chota Nagpur Plateau, the park features mixed deciduous forests (sal, bamboo, mahua), grasslands beside the North Koel River and its tributaries, scenic valleys, hills and several waterfalls including Lodh Falls (~468 feet).

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Bengal tiger, leopard, elephant, gaur (bison), chital, sambar, sloth bear, wolf, jackal, wild boar, mouse deer, Indian pangolin, civet, langur, Indian giant squirrel, four‑horned antelope, jungle cat, mongoose and more.
  • Birdlife: Over 180‑plus species including peafowl, hornbills, drongos, forest owlet, crested serpent eagle, black ibis, swamp hen, quail, harial and migratory waterbirds around Kamaldah Lake.
  • Flora: Dominated by sal and bamboo, interspersed with mahua, palash and medicinal plants across varied terrain.

Activities

  • Jeep safaris (guided) into core and buffer zones for wildlife sightings
  • Elephant safaris early morning in select zones
  • Bird‑watching from watchtowers or forest trails
  • Visits to nearby attractions like Lodh Falls, Palamu Fort ruins and Netarhat hills.

Accessibility & Accommodation

Located ~25 km south of Medininagar, ~65 km northwest of Latehar and ~170 km from Ranchi. Entry is through the forest department zones of Palamau Tiger Reserve. Tourist lodges, forest rest houses, log huts and tree houses are available within and around Betla.

Significance

One of India's first nine tiger reserves under Project Tiger (1974), and site of the world’s first tiger census conducted via pug‑marks in 1932. A unique blend of biodiversity and historical heritage within the only national park in Jharkhand.
