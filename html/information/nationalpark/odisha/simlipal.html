Simlipal National Park

Location & Area: Located in Mayurbhanj district, Odisha; spans ~2750 km² including reserve forest. Declared a National Park in 1980 and a Tiger Reserve under Project Tiger.

Geography & Habitat

Part of the Eastern Highlands moist deciduous forest. Home to hills, valleys, waterfalls (like Barehipani and Joranda), and dense sal forests. Elevation ranges from 200 to 1185 meters.

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Bengal tigers, Asian elephants, Indian bison (gaur), leopards, giant squirrels, and over 300 bird species including the hill myna.
  • Flora: Dominated by sal trees; over 1,076 plant species including medicinal herbs, orchids, and creepers.

Activities

  • Jungle safari and nature trails
  • Visit to Barehipani and Joranda waterfalls
  • Birdwatching and tribal cultural experience

Accessibility & Permits

Accessible via Baripada (22 km); permits issued by Forest Department; best visited between November to June.

Significance

Part of UNESCO World Network of Biosphere Reserves since 2009. Rich biodiversity and cultural heritage — home to many tribal communities.
