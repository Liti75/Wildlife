Nilgiri Biosphere Reserve

Location & Area: Spread across Tamil Nadu, Kerala, and Karnataka; covering ~5,520 km² in the Western Ghats.

Geography & Habitat

Comprising the Nilgiri Hills, Silent Valley, Wayanad, Bandipur, Nagarhole, and Mudumalai areas, the reserve is marked by montane forests, sholas, grasslands, and high biodiversity.

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Asian elephant, tiger, leopard, lion-tailed macaque, gaur, dhole, and over 300 bird species.
  • Flora: Evergreen and moist deciduous forests, teak, rosewood, and numerous medicinal plants.

Activities

  • Wildlife safaris in Bandipur, Nagarhole, and Mudumalai
  • Trekking in Silent Valley and Wayanad
  • Birdwatching in high-altitude shola forests

Accessibility & Permits

Nearest towns: Ooty, Mysore, and Kozhikode. Accessible by road and rail; permits required in protected core areas.

Significance

A UNESCO World Network of Biosphere Reserve site, vital for elephant and tiger corridors in the Western Ghats.
