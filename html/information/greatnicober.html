Great Nicobar Biosphere Reserve

Location & Area: Situated on Great Nicobar Island, Andaman & Nicobar Islands, covering ~885 km² of tropical rainforests and coastal ecosystems.

Geography & Habitat

The reserve includes pristine tropical evergreen forests, mangroves, coral reefs, and sandy beaches, providing a unique blend of marine and terrestrial ecosystems.

Flora & Fauna

  • Fauna: Saltwater crocodiles, Nicobar megapode, giant robber crab, leatherback turtles, and endemic bird species.
  • Flora: Rich tropical rainforest vegetation with many endemic orchids, palms, and mangrove species.

Activities

  • Wildlife observation and trekking
  • Exploration of mangroves and coastal habitats
  • Marine biodiversity studies

Accessibility & Permits

Accessible mainly via ship or air to Port Blair, followed by permits and transport to Great Nicobar. Entry permits are mandatory for visitors.

Significance

A UNESCO Biosphere Reserve, it plays a vital role in conserving unique island ecosystems, endangered species, and protecting indigenous cultures.
